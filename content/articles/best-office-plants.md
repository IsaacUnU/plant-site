## African Violet: The Low-Light Superstar
The African Violet is a popular choice for office plants, and for good reason. This beautiful, compact plant is capable of producing stunning, delicate blooms in a range of colors, from soft pastels to vibrant purples and blues. But what really sets the African Violet apart is its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it perfect for offices with limited natural light.

Why we recommend it: The African Violet is a low-maintenance plant that requires only 1-2 hours of direct sunlight per day and can tolerate temperatures as low as 65°F (18°C). It's also relatively small, reaching a maximum height of 8-10 inches (20-25 cm), making it perfect for small desks or shelves.

## Frequently Asked Questions
Q: What is the best way to water my office plants?
A: The best way to water your office plants is to water them thoroughly, allowing the soil to dry slightly between waterings. This will help prevent overwatering and root rot.
Q: How often should I fertilize my office plants?
A: You should fertilize your office plants every 1-2 months, using a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer. This will help provide your plants with the nutrients they need to thrive.
Q: Can I keep my office plants in a room with no natural light?
A: Yes, many office plants can thrive in rooms with no natural light, as long as they are provided with sufficient artificial lighting. Look for plants that are specifically designed to tolerate low-light conditions, such as the African Violet or the Pothos.
