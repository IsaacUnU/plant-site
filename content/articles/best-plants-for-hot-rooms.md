## What Makes a Plant Heat-Tolerant?
Before we dive into the list of plants, it's essential to understand what makes a plant heat-tolerant. Heat-tolerant plants have adapted to survive in warm temperatures, often with unique characteristics such as thick leaves, deep roots, or specialized photosynthetic pathways. These adaptations enable them to conserve water, protect themselves from heat stress, and maintain their growth and development in warm conditions. By choosing plants with these characteristics, you can create a thriving indoor garden that can withstand the heat.

## African Violet: The Low-Light Superstar
African Violets are one of the most popular houseplants, and for good reason. They're easy to care for, produce beautiful flowers, and can thrive in low-light conditions. But what makes them truly special is their ability to tolerate warm temperatures, up to 75°F (24°C). With proper care, African Violets can bloom for 6-9 months, producing an average of 10-15 flowers per plant.

Why we recommend it: African Violets are an excellent choice for warm rooms because they can survive with minimal watering, requiring only 1-2 cups of water per week. They also have a unique trait called "photoperiodism," which allows them to adjust their growth and flowering to the surrounding light conditions. This means that they can thrive in low-light conditions, making them perfect for rooms with limited natural light.

Quick Stats:
* Light: Indirect light (50-75% shade)
* Water: 1-2 cups per week
* Difficulty: Easy
* Pet Safe: No

## Chinese Evergreen: A Versatile and Low-Maintenance Option
Chinese Evergreen is a popular houseplant that's known for its beautiful leaves and low-maintenance care. It can thrive in a range of lighting conditions, from low to bright indirect light, and can tolerate temperatures up to 80°F (27°C). With proper care, Chinese Evergreen can grow up to 3 feet tall and produce beautiful white or yellow flowers.

Why we recommend it: Chinese Evergreen is an excellent choice for warm rooms because it's extremely versatile and can adapt to different lighting conditions. It also has a unique trait called "variegation," which means that its leaves have different colors and patterns. This makes it a beautiful addition to any room, and its low-maintenance care requirements make it perfect for busy plant enthusiasts.
