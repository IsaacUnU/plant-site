## Aloe Vera: The Medicinal Marvel
Aloe Vera is a popular choice for indoor gardens, and for good reason. This succulent plant is not only easy to care for but also boasts a range of medicinal benefits. With its thick, fleshy leaves that can store up to 95% water, Aloe Vera can survive for weeks without water. But what really sets it apart is its ability to thrive in indirect-bright light, making it perfect for rooms with limited natural light.

Why we recommend it: Aloe Vera is a low-maintenance plant that can tolerate a wide range of temperatures (between 65-80°F) and humidity levels (between 40-60%). It's also a great air purifier, removing toxins like formaldehyde and benzene from the air. With its unique ability to store water, Aloe Vera can go without water for up to 2 weeks, making it perfect for busy people or those who tend to forget to water their plants.

## Frequently Asked Questions
Q: How often should I water my drought-tolerant houseplants?
A: The frequency of watering will depend on the specific plant and its needs. However, as a general rule, drought-tolerant houseplants can survive with minimal watering, often every 1-2 weeks.
Q: Can I leave my drought-tolerant houseplants outside in the sun?
A: While some drought-tolerant houseplants can tolerate direct sunlight, others may require shade or indirect light. Be sure to research the specific needs of your plant to ensure it's getting the right amount of light.
Q: Are drought-tolerant houseplants pet-friendly?
A: Unfortunately, many drought-tolerant houseplants are toxic to pets, so it's essential to keep them out of reach of your furry friends. Be sure to research the toxicity of your plant before bringing it into your home.
