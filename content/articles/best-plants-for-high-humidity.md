## The Hidden Dangers of Low Humidity for Houseplants
If you're an avid houseplant enthusiast, you're likely aware of the importance of maintaining optimal humidity levels for your plants. However, many of us underestimate the risks associated with low humidity. Dry air can cause leaves to become brittle and prone to breakage, while also making plants more susceptible to pests and diseases. In fact, a humidity level below 40% can lead to a 25% increase in transpiration, causing your plants to lose precious water and nutrients. To avoid these issues, it's essential to choose plants that thrive in high humidity environments.

## Frequently Asked Questions
Q: What is the ideal humidity level for houseplants?
A: The ideal humidity level for houseplants varies depending on the species, but most plants prefer a relative humidity of 40-60%.
Q: Can I use a humidifier to increase the humidity around my plants?
A: Yes, using a humidifier can help increase the humidity around your plants, but be sure to monitor the humidity level to avoid over-humidification.
Q: Are all the plants on this list pet-safe?
A: No, not all the plants on this list are pet-safe. Be sure to check the pet safety information for each plant before introducing it to your home if you have pets.
