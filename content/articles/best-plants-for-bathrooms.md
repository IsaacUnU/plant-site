## Alocasia Polly: The low-light superstar
Alocasia Polly, also known as Alocasia x amazonica, is a stunning plant that thrives in low-light conditions, making it perfect for bathrooms with limited natural light. This plant is known for its beautiful, heart-shaped leaves with intricate vein patterns and can grow up to 2 feet tall. With proper care, Alocasia Polly can survive in temperatures as low as 55°F (13°C) and as high as 85°F (29°C), making it a great option for bathrooms with temperature fluctuations.

Why we recommend it: Alocasia Polly is a low-maintenance plant that requires only 1-2 cups of water per week, making it perfect for busy people who don't have a lot of time to care for their plants. Additionally, this plant can tolerate high humidity levels, up to 80%, which is typical in most bathrooms. With its beautiful foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, Alocasia Polly is an excellent choice for bathrooms with limited natural light.

Quick Stats:
* Light: Indirect light
* Water: 1-2 cups per week
* Difficulty: Easy
* Pet Safe: No

## Frequently Asked Questions
Q: What is the best way to care for my bathroom plants?
A: The best way to care for your bathroom plants is to provide them with the right amount of light, water, and humidity. Make sure to research the specific care requirements for each plant to ensure you're providing the best conditions for them to thrive.
Q: Can I use tap water to water my bathroom plants?
A: It's generally recommended to use filtered or distilled water for your bathroom plants, as tap water can contain high levels of minerals and chemicals that can be harmful to your plants.
Q: How often should I fertilize my bathroom plants?
A: The frequency of fertilization will depend on the specific plant and its growth rate. As a general rule, fertilize your plants during the growing season (spring and summer) with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer. Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to avoid burning your plants' roots.
