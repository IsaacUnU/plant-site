## Overview
Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum, also known as Star Jasmine, is a popular evergreen climber native to China and Vietnam. It has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers in the spring. As a houseplant, it's valued for its ability to purify the air and its pleasant scent, making it a great addition to any room. In its natural habitat, Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum grows in moist, well-draining soil and partial shade, which makes it a great choice for rooms with bright, indirect light.

The scientific name, Jasminum polyanthum, refers to the plant's many-flowered inflorescences. It's a member of the Oleaceae family, which includes other popular houseplants like olive trees and ash trees. With proper care, Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum can grow up to 20 feet tall, making it a great choice for trellises or arbors. However, it can be pruned to maintain a smaller size, making it suitable for containers or indoor spaces.

Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum is a popular choice for houseplants because it's relatively low maintenance and can thrive in a variety of conditions. ## Light: The Window Direction That Determines Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum's Fate
Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum prefers bright, indirect light, which makes it a great choice for rooms with east- or west-facing windows. The ideal light range is 200-400 foot-candles, which is similar to the light produced by a north-facing window. To determine the best window direction, consider the following:
- East-facing windows receive gentle, indirect light in the morning, making them ideal for Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum.
- West-facing windows receive bright, indirect light in the afternoon, which can be too intense for Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum.
- South-facing windows receive direct sunlight, which can be too intense for Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum.
- North-facing windows receive low light, which can be too dim for Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum.

## Watering: How to Water Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum Without Drowning It
Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum prefers moist but not waterlogged soil, which makes it essential to water it carefully. Here are three methods to check the soil moisture:
1. Finger test: Stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle to check the moisture level.
2. Pot weight: Check the weight of the pot before and after watering to ensure it's not too heavy or too light.
3. Moisture meter: Use a moisture meter to check the soil moisture level, which can be more accurate than the finger test.

The exact watering frequency depends on the season, with more frequent watering in the summer and less frequent watering in the winter. As a general rule, water Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Soil Mix Makes Root Rot Rare
Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum prefers a well-draining potting mix that's rich in organic matter. A good soil recipe is:
- 60% potting mix
- 30% perlite
- 10% orchid bark

This mix provides good drainage and aeration, which can help prevent root rot. The pot material is also crucial, as it can affect the soil moisture level. Terracotta pots are a good choice, as they allow for good drainage and aeration.

To repot Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum, follow these steps:
1. Choose a pot that's only 1-2 sizes larger than the current one.
2. Prepare the new pot by adding a layer of potting mix at the bottom.
3. Gently remove the plant from the old pot and trim any dead or damaged roots.
4. Place the plant in the new pot and add fresh potting mix around the roots.
5. Water the plant thoroughly after repotting.

## Fertilizing
Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum prefers a balanced fertilizer that's rich in nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. A good fertilizer is a 10-10-10 NPK ratio, which provides equal amounts of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Fertilize the plant monthly during the spring and summer, and dilute the fertilizer to half strength to avoid burning the roots.

## Humidity and Temperature
Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum prefers a humidity level of 50-70%, which can be challenging to maintain in dry climates. Here are some tips to maintain the right humidity level:
- Use a pebble tray: Place the pot on a tray filled with water and pebbles to increase the humidity around the plant.
- Use a humidifier: Place a humidifier near the plant to maintain a consistent humidity level.
- Group plants: Grouping plants together can increase the humidity around them, as they release moisture into the air.

The ideal temperature range is 65-80°F (18-27°C), which makes it a great choice for rooms with average temperatures. Avoid placing the plant near heating vents or cold windows, as extreme temperatures can be detrimental to its health.

### Yellowing Leaves
If the leaves of your Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum are turning yellow, it's likely due to overwatering. To fix this, reduce the watering frequency and make sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. You can also prune the plant to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.

### Droopy Stems
If the stems of your Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum are droopy, it's likely due to underwatering. To fix this, increase the watering frequency and make sure the soil is consistently moist. You can also prune the plant to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.

### White Powdery Patches
If you notice white powdery patches on the leaves of your Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum, it's likely due to powdery mildew. To fix this, treat the plant with a fungicide and improve air circulation around the plant. You can also prune the plant to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.

### Holes in Leaves
If you notice holes in the leaves of your Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum, it's likely due to pests. To fix this, treat the plant with insecticidal soap and inspect the plant regularly for pests. You can also prune the plant to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.

### Slow Growth
If your Jasmine Jasminum polyanthum is growing slowly, it's likely due to a lack of light. To fix this, move the plant to a brighter location and prune it to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth. You can also fertilize the plant with a balanced fertilizer to promote healthy growth.
