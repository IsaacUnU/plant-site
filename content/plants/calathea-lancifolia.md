## Quick Care Summary

To care for your Calathea lancifolia, provide it with bright, indirect light, and water it weekly during the spring and summer months, reducing frequency to every 10-14 days during the fall and winter. Maintain a consistent temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and keep the humidity level at a moderate 50-60%. This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a great option for beginners. With proper care, your Calathea lancifolia will thrive and bring a touch of tropical beauty to your home.

## Light Requirements

The Calathea lancifolia prefers bright, indirect light, but can tolerate low light conditions. East- or west-facing windows are ideal, as they provide gentle, indirect light. Avoid placing the plant in direct sunlight, as this can cause the leaves to become scorched. If you're unsure about the light levels in your home, you can measure the foot-candles using a light meter. The Calathea lancifolia prefers light levels between 200-400 foot-candles.

## Watering

To water your Calathea lancifolia, start by checking the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water the plant thoroughly, making sure the pot drains well to prevent waterlogged soil. During the spring and summer months, water your Calathea lancifolia weekly. Reduce the frequency to every 10-14 days during the fall and winter months. Use room-temperature water, as cold water can shock the roots. Avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent fungal diseases.

## Soil and Potting

The Calathea lancifolia prefers a well-draining potting mix that is rich in organic matter. A mix of 2 parts peat, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark is ideal. Use a pot that is slightly larger than the plant's root system, as this will give the roots room to grow. Repot your Calathea lancifolia every 2-3 years, as the plant will outgrow its container. Signs that your plant needs to be repotted include the soil becoming packed and the plant becoming top-heavy.

## Fertilizing

Fertilize your Calathea lancifolia during the growing season (spring and summer) with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer. Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. A fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 20-20-20 is ideal. Apply the fertilizer once a month, taking care to avoid getting any on the leaves.

## Humidity and Temperature

The Calathea lancifolia prefers a moderate to high humidity level, between 50-60%. To achieve this, you can use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles. As the water evaporates, it will increase the humidity around the plant. Maintain a consistent temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C), avoiding any sudden fluctuations. Keep the plant away from heating or cooling vents, fireplaces, and drafty windows.

## Common Problems

### Yellowing Leaves
The symptom: Yellowing leaves can be a sign of over-watering or under-watering. The cause: If the soil is too wet, the roots will rot, causing the leaves to turn yellow. If the soil is too dry, the plant will not be able to absorb enough water, also causing the leaves to turn yellow. The fix: Check the soil moisture and adjust your watering schedule accordingly. Make sure the pot drains well and avoid getting water on the leaves.

### Brown Leaves
The symptom: Brown leaves can be a sign of underwatering or exposure to direct sunlight. The cause: If the soil is too dry, the plant will not be able to absorb enough water, causing the leaves to turn brown. If the plant is exposed to direct sunlight, the leaves can become scorched, also turning brown. The fix: Check the soil moisture and adjust your watering schedule accordingly. Move the plant to a spot with bright, indirect light.

### Pests
The symptom: Pests, such as spider mites, mealybugs, or scale, can cause damage to the plant. The cause: Pests can be introduced to the plant through contaminated soil or by bringing infested plants into the home. The fix: Inspect the plant regularly for signs of pests and treat promptly if necessary. Use insecticidal soap or neem oil to control infestations.

### Root Rot
The symptom: Root rot can cause the plant to become weak and leggy, with yellowing or browning leaves. The cause: Over-watering can cause the roots to rot, leading to root rot. The fix: Check the soil moisture and adjust your watering schedule accordingly. Repot the plant in fresh, well-draining soil and reduce watering frequency.

### Leaf Drop
The symptom: Leaf drop can be a sign of stress or shock. The cause: Sudden changes in temperature, humidity, or light can cause the plant to drop its leaves. The fix: Maintain a consistent environment and avoid sudden changes. Check the soil moisture and adjust your watering schedule accordingly.

## Propagation

To propagate your Calathea lancifolia, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, making sure it has at least two nodes.
2. Remove the lower leaves, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Place the stem section in a glass of water or a propagation tray filled with water.
4. Keep the water level topped up and change the water every few days to prevent bacterial growth.
5. Roots should develop within 1-2 weeks, at which point you can plant the stem section in a well-draining potting mix.
6. Keep the soil consistently moist and provide bright, indirect light.
