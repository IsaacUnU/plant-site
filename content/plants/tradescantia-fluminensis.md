## Quick Care Summary
To care for Tradescantia fluminensis, provide it with indirect light, ideally between 200-400 foot-candles, and water it every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and maintain a medium humidity level of 50-70%. This plant is relatively easy to care for and can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a great choice for beginners. With proper care, Tradescantia fluminensis can grow up to 3 feet long and produce small, white flowers in the spring and summer.

Tradescantia fluminensis is also known for its ability to purify the air, making it a great choice for indoor spaces.

## Light: The Importance of Indirect Light for Tradescantia fluminensis
Tradescantia fluminensis prefers indirect light, ideally between 200-400 foot-candles. Direct sunlight can cause the leaves to become scorched and discolored, while low light can lead to weak and leggy growth. The best window orientation for Tradescantia fluminensis is east- or west-facing, as these directions provide gentle, indirect light. In the Northern Hemisphere, a north-facing window can also provide suitable light, but may require additional lighting during the winter months.

## Watering: How to Water Tradescantia fluminensis Without Drowning It
Tradescantia fluminensis prefers moist soil, but can be sensitive to over-watering. To avoid drowning the plant, check the soil moisture by sticking your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water the plant thoroughly, making sure the pot drains well to prevent waterlogged soil. In the summer, water the plant every 7-10 days, and in the winter, reduce watering to every 14 days.

## Soil & Repotting: The Importance of Well-Draining Soil for Tradescantia fluminensis
Tradescantia fluminensis prefers well-draining soil to prevent waterlogged soil and root rot. A good soil recipe is 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark. This mix provides good drainage and aeration, while also retaining moisture and nutrients. Repot the plant every 2-3 years to maintain soil quality and provide a larger pot if necessary.

## Fertilizing
Tradescantia fluminensis benefits from regular fertilization, especially during the growing season. Use a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer, diluted to half strength, and apply it monthly. This will provide the plant with the necessary nutrients to promote healthy growth and flowering.

## Humidity and Temperature
Tradescantia fluminensis prefers a medium humidity level of 50-70% and a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). To maintain the right humidity level, use a humidifier or group plants together to create a microclimate. Avoid placing the plant near heating or cooling vents, as this can cause temperature fluctuations and dry out the air.

## Toxicity & Safety: Is Tradescantia fluminensis Safe for Pets and Children?
Tradescantia fluminensis is non-toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA. However, it's still important to keep the plant out of reach of pets and children to avoid accidental ingestion. If you suspect your pet has ingested any part of the plant, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435 for advice.

### Over-Watering
Over-watering is a common problem with Tradescantia fluminensis, as it can be sensitive to too much moisture. To fix this problem, reduce watering frequency and make sure the pot drains well to prevent waterlogged soil.

### Under-Watering
Under-watering can also be a problem, especially during the summer months when the plant is actively growing. To fix this problem, increase watering frequency and make sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

### Mealybugs
Mealybugs are small, white, cottony pests that can infest Tradescantia fluminensis. To treat mealybugs, use insecticidal soap and make sure to inspect the plant regularly for signs of infestation.

### Spider Mites
Spider mites are small, spider-like pests that can infest Tradescantia fluminensis. To treat spider mites, use neem oil and make sure to increase humidity and reduce stress on the plant.

## Propagation
Tradescantia fluminensis can be propagated through stem cuttings. To propagate, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem from the mother plant, just above a node.
2. Remove lower leaves from the cutting, leaving only a few leaves at the top.
3. Place the cutting in a glass of water or a propagation tray filled with water.
4. Keep the water level topped up and change the water every few days.
5. Roots should develop within 1-2 weeks, and the cutting can be potted up into a well-draining soil mix.
