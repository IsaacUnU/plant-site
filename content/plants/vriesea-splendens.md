## Quick Care Summary
To care for Vriesea splendens, provide it with bright, indirect light, ideally 200-400 foot-candles, and maintain a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). Water it every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, using room-temperature, filtered water. This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it suitable for beginners. With proper care, Vriesea splendens can thrive and even help purify the air, as a 1989 NASA Clean Air Study found it effective at reducing formaldehyde in enclosed spaces.

## Overview
The Vriesea splendens, a member of the bromeliad family, is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. In its natural habitat, it can be found growing on trees, rocks, and other surfaces, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves. This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its striking appearance, with bright yellow and red flowers that can last for several months. The scientific name, Vriesea splendens, reflects its vibrant and showy nature.

The Vriesea splendens is a relatively small plant, growing up to 2 feet (60 cm) in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often a deep green color. The plant's most distinctive feature, however, is its bright yellow and red flower spike, which can grow up to 12 inches (30 cm) in height. This flower spike is not only beautiful but also serves as a natural indicator of the plant's overall health.

In addition to its aesthetic appeal, the Vriesea splendens is also a low-maintenance plant that can thrive in a variety of conditions. It is relatively pest- and disease-free, and can tolerate some neglect, making it an ideal choice for busy plant enthusiasts.

## Light: The Importance of Bright, Indirect Light
The Vriesea splendens requires bright, indirect light to thrive, ideally 200-400 foot-candles. This can be achieved by placing the plant 3-5 feet (0.9-1.5 meters) away from an east- or west-facing window. Direct sunlight can cause the leaves to become scorched, so it's essential to provide filtered or dappled light. In the winter months, when the days are shorter and the light is less intense, the plant may require more frequent watering to compensate for the reduced light.

## Watering: How to Water Vriesea splendens Without Drowning It
To water the Vriesea splendens, use room-temperature, filtered water, and water the plant in the morning to allow the soil to dry slightly before nightfall. Water the plant every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, or when the top 1-2 inches (2.5-5 cm) of soil feel dry to the touch. Avoid getting water on the plant's leaves or crown to prevent rot.

## Soil & Repotting: The Importance of Well-Draining Soil
The Vriesea splendens requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. A mix of 60% potting mix + 30% perlite + 10% orchid bark is ideal. Repot the plant every 2-3 years to maintain soil quality and provide a larger pot if necessary. Choose a pot that is only 1-2 sizes larger than the current one to prevent the soil from becoming too wet.

## Fertilizing
Fertilize the Vriesea splendens with a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer, diluted to half strength, during the growing season (spring-summer). Avoid fertilizing during the winter months when the plant is dormant.

## Humidity and Temperature
Maintain a humid environment, ideally 50-70%, by using a pebble tray or a humidifier. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) to prevent stress to the plant. Avoid placing the plant near heating or cooling vents, fireplaces, or drafty windows to maintain a stable temperature.

## Toxicity & Safety: Non-Toxic to Cats and Dogs
The Vriesea splendens is non-toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA. However, it's still essential to keep the plant out of reach of pets to prevent any potential allergic reactions or digestive issues. If ingested, the plant may cause mild gastrointestinal upset, but it is not toxic. In case of ingestion, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435 for advice.

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
The most likely cause of a soft, mushy stem base is overwatering. To fix this, reduce the watering frequency and repot the plant in well-draining soil. To prevent this issue, water the plant carefully, avoiding getting water on the plant's leaves or crown.

### Yellowing Leaves
Yellowing leaves can be caused by underwatering. To fix this, increase the watering frequency, but avoid overwatering. To prevent this issue, water the plant regularly, but make sure not to overwater.

### Brown Tips on Leaves
Brown tips on leaves can be caused by dry air. To fix this, increase the humidity around the plant. To prevent this issue, use a pebble tray or a humidifier to maintain a humid environment.

### Pests on the Plant
Pests on the plant can be caused by infestation. To fix this, treat the plant with insecticidal soap. To prevent this issue, inspect the plant regularly, and isolate new plants for 2-3 weeks before introducing them to the main collection.

### Slow Growth
Slow growth can be caused by a lack of light. To fix this, move the plant to a brighter location. To prevent this issue, provide bright, indirect light, and avoid direct sunlight.

## Propagation
To propagate Vriesea splendens, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, just below a node.
2. Remove lower leaves, leaving only 2-3 leaves at the top.
3. Plant the stem section in a well-draining potting mix, and water thoroughly.
4. Place the plant in a bright, humid location, and maintain a temperature of 65-80°F (18-27°C).
5. Roots should develop within 1-2 months, and the new plant can be repotted in a larger container.
