## Light Requirements
The Alocasia dragon scale prefers bright, indirect light, but it can also thrive in low light conditions. The ideal light range for this plant is 200-400 foot-candles. If you're placing your Alocasia dragon scale near a window, make sure it's at least 3-4 feet away to avoid direct sunlight, which can cause the leaves to become scorched. Signs of too much light include yellowing or browning of the leaves, while too little light can cause the plant to become leggy.

## Watering
To water your Alocasia dragon scale, follow these steps:
1. Check the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water.
2. Water your Alocasia dragon scale once a week during the summer months and every 10-14 days during the winter.
3. Use room-temperature water to avoid shocking the roots.
4. Avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent fungal diseases.
5. Allow the pot to drain thoroughly to prevent waterlogged soil.

Signs of over-watering include yellowing or droopy leaves, while under-watering can cause the leaves to become crispy and brown. To prevent these issues, make sure to check the soil moisture regularly and adjust your watering schedule accordingly.

## Soil and Potting
The Alocasia dragon scale prefers a well-draining potting mix that's rich in organic matter. A recommended soil mix recipe is 2 parts peat, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark. This mix will provide the right amount of moisture and drainage for your plant. When it comes to potting, choose a container that's at least 1-2 sizes larger than the plant's current pot to give the roots room to grow. Repot your Alocasia dragon scale every 1-2 years to refresh the soil and provide a larger pot if necessary. Signs that repotting is needed include the plant becoming top-heavy or the soil becoming depleted of nutrients.

## Fertilizing
Feed your Alocasia dragon scale with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer (20-20-20 NPK) during the growing season (spring and summer). Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. Fertilize your plant once a month, taking a break during the winter months when the plant is dormant.

## Common Problems
### Over-Watering
Symptom: Yellowing or droopy leaves
Cause: Too much water in the soil
Fix: Allow the soil to dry out slightly between waterings, and adjust your watering schedule accordingly.

### Under-Watering
Symptom: Crispy or brown leaves
Cause: Not enough water in the soil
Fix: Increase the frequency of your waterings, and make sure to check the soil moisture regularly.

### Low Humidity
Symptom: Brown or crispy leaves
Cause: Low humidity level
Fix: Use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles to increase the surrounding humidity.

### Pests
Symptom: Small, moving dots on the leaves or stems
Cause: Infestation of pests such as spider mites, mealybugs, or scale
Fix: Isolate the plant, and treat it with insecticidal soap or neem oil.

### Root Rot
Symptom: Soft, mushy stems or roots
Cause: Over-watering or poor drainage
Fix: Repot the plant in fresh, well-draining soil, and adjust your watering schedule accordingly.

### Leaf Drop
Symptom: Leaves falling off the plant
Cause: Changes in temperature, humidity, or light
Fix: Maintain a consistent environment, and adjust your care routine accordingly.

## Propagation
To propagate your Alocasia dragon scale, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, making sure it has at least two nodes.
2. Remove lower leaves from the stem, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Place the stem in a glass of water or a propagation tray filled with water.
4. Keep the water level topped up, and change the water every 1-2 weeks to prevent bacterial growth.
5. Roots should develop within 1-2 weeks, and you can transplant the new plant into a pot filled with well-draining soil.
The best time to propagate is during the spring and summer months when the plant is actively growing.
