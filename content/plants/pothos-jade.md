## Quick Care Summary
To care for your Pothos jade, provide it with bright, indirect light, and water it once a week during the summer months and every 10-14 days during the winter. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and maintain a medium humidity level. As an easy-to-care-for plant, the Pothos jade is perfect for beginners, requiring minimal maintenance and attention. With proper care, it can thrive and become a beautiful addition to any room.

The plant's ability to grow in a variety of lighting conditions, from low to bright indirect light, makes it a versatile choice for indoor spaces. In its native habitat, the Pothos jade can grow quite large, but when grown indoors, it typically reaches a height of 3-6 feet.

As a low-maintenance plant, the Pothos jade is perfect for busy people or those new to plant care. ## Light Requirements
The Pothos jade prefers bright, indirect light, but can tolerate low light conditions. It's best to place it near an east- or west-facing window, where it can receive gentle, indirect light. Avoid placing it in direct sunlight, as this can cause the leaves to become scorched. The ideal light intensity for the Pothos jade is between 200-400 foot-candles. If you're unsure about the light intensity in your space, you can use a light meter to determine the best spot for your plant.

## Watering
To water your Pothos jade, follow these steps:
1. Check the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water.
2. Water your plant once a week during the summer months and every 10-14 days during the winter.
3. Use room-temperature water, as cold water can shock the roots.
4. Avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent fungal diseases.
5. Make sure the pot has good drainage holes to prevent waterlogged soil.

## Soil and Potting
The Pothos jade prefers a well-draining potting mix that's rich in organic matter. A good recipe for a potting mix is 2 parts peat, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark. This mix will provide your plant with the right amount of moisture and nutrients. When it comes to potting, choose a container that's slightly larger than the plant's root system. Repot your Pothos jade every 2-3 years, or when the plant becomes pot-bound.

## Fertilizing
To fertilize your Pothos jade, use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 20-20-20. Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. Feed your plant once a month during the growing season (spring and summer), and skip fertilizing during the dormant season (fall and winter).

## Humidity and Temperature
The Pothos jade prefers a medium humidity level, between 40-60%. To achieve this, you can use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles. The temperature range for the Pothos jade is between 65-80°F (18-27°C). Avoid placing your plant near heating or cooling vents, fireplaces, or drafty windows.

## Common Problems
### Overwatering
Symptom: Yellowing leaves, droopy stems, and soft, mushy roots.
Cause: Too much water, poor drainage, or high humidity.
Fix: Reduce watering frequency, improve drainage, and provide better air circulation.

### Underwatering
Symptom: Wilting leaves, dry, crispy stems, and brown edges.
Cause: Infrequent watering, low humidity, or high temperatures.
Fix: Increase watering frequency, provide more humidity, and maintain a consistent temperature.

### Pests
Symptom: Tiny, moving dots on the leaves or stems.
Cause: Infestation by spider mites, mealybugs, or aphids.
Fix: Isolate the plant, treat with insecticidal soap, and improve air circulation.

### Leaf Drop
Symptom: Leaves falling off the plant, often due to changes in temperature, humidity, or light.
Cause: Sudden changes in the environment, overwatering, or underwatering.
Fix: Maintain a consistent environment, adjust watering and fertilizing schedules, and provide support for the plant.

### Root Rot
Symptom: Soft, mushy roots, and a foul odor.
Cause: Overwatering, poor drainage, or high humidity.
Fix: Reduce watering frequency, improve drainage, and provide better air circulation.

### Nutrient Deficiency
Symptom: Yellowing leaves, stunted growth, or poor flower production.
Cause: Lack of essential nutrients, such as nitrogen, iron, or magnesium.
Fix: Fertilize with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer, and adjust the pH level of the soil if necessary.

## Propagation
To propagate your Pothos jade, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section with at least two nodes.
2. Remove lower leaves, leaving only the top two or three.
3. Place the cutting in a glass of water or a propagation tray filled with water.
4. Keep the water level topped up and change the water every 2-3 days.
5. Roots should develop within 1-2 weeks, and the new plant can be transferred to soil after 2-3 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**How often should I water my Pothos jade?**
Water your Pothos jade once a week during the summer months and every 10-14 days during the winter. Make sure to check the soil moisture before watering, and avoid getting water on the leaves.

**Is Pothos jade toxic to cats and dogs?**
No, the Pothos jade is non-toxic to cats and dogs, making it a great choice for pet owners.

**Why are the leaves on my Pothos jade turning yellow?**
Yellowing leaves can be caused by overwatering, underwatering, or a lack of nutrients. Check the soil moisture, adjust your watering schedule, and fertilize your plant to prevent yellowing leaves.

**Can Pothos jade grow in low light?**
Yes, the Pothos jade can tolerate low light conditions, but it will do best in bright, indirect light. If you're placing your plant in a low-light area, make sure to adjust your watering and fertilizing schedules accordingly.

**How big does Pothos jade get indoors?**
The Pothos jade can grow quite large indoors, typically reaching a height of 3-6 feet. With regular pruning, you can keep your plant compact and bushy, or let it trail down from a hanging basket.
