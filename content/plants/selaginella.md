## Quick Care Summary
To care for Selaginella, provide it with bright, indirect light, and maintain a consistent temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C). Water your Selaginella every 7-10 days during the summer and every 14 days during the winter, making sure not to overwater. With its forgiving nature and ability to thrive in medium humidity, Selaginella is an excellent choice for beginners. A 1989 NASA Clean Air Study found Selaginella effective at reducing formaldehyde in enclosed spaces, making it a great addition to any indoor space.

## Overview
Selaginella uncinata, commonly known as Spike Moss, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. In its natural habitat, Selaginella grows on rocks, trees, and other surfaces, forming dense mats of moss-like foliage. This unique plant has gained popularity as a houseplant due to its low-maintenance requirements and striking appearance. The deep green, spike-like foliage of Selaginella adds a touch of exotic beauty to any room, making it a popular choice for plant enthusiasts.

Selaginella is a relatively small plant, typically growing up to 6-8 inches (15-20 cm) in height and 12-18 inches (30-45 cm) in width. Its compact size and slow growth rate make it an ideal choice for small spaces, such as apartments or offices. With proper care, Selaginella can thrive in indoor conditions, providing a beautiful and air-purifying addition to any room.

## Light: The Importance of Indirect Light for Selaginella
Selaginella requires bright, indirect light to thrive. The ideal foot-candle range for optimal growth is 200-400 foot-candles. Place Selaginella 3-5 feet away from an east- or west-facing window to provide the right amount of light. Avoid direct sunlight, as it can cause the leaves to become scorched or discolored. During the winter months, when the sun is lower in the sky, you may need to adjust the plant's position to ensure it receives sufficient light.

## Watering: The Delicate Balance of Moisture for Selaginella
Watering Selaginella can be a delicate balance. Overwatering can lead to root rot, while underwatering can cause the leaves to become dry and brittle. Check the soil moisture by sticking your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water Selaginella every 7-10 days during the summer and every 14 days during the winter. Use room-temperature water, and avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent fungal diseases.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Mix for Selaginella
Selaginella requires a well-draining potting mix to prevent waterlogged soil. A mix of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark provides the right balance of moisture and drainage. Repot Selaginella every 2-3 years to maintain soil quality and provide a larger pot if necessary. Choose a pot that is only slightly larger than the previous one, as Selaginella prefers to be slightly root-bound.

## Fertilizing
Feed Selaginella with a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer during the spring and summer months. Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. Apply the fertilizer once a month, and skip fertilizing during the winter months when the plant is dormant.

## Humidity and Temperature
Selaginella requires a humid environment to thrive, with an ideal humidity range of 50-70%. Use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles to maintain a humid microclimate. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C), avoiding sudden fluctuations. Avoid placing Selaginella near heating vents or cold windows, as this can cause stress to the plant.

## Toxicity & Safety: A Safe Choice for Pet Owners
According to the ASPCA, Selaginella is non-toxic to cats and dogs, making it a safe choice for pet owners. However, it's still possible for pets to experience gastrointestinal upset if they ingest a large amount of the plant. Keep Selaginella out of reach of pets and children to avoid any potential problems. If you suspect your pet has ingested Selaginella, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435 for advice.

## Common Problems
### Yellowing Leaves
Yellowing leaves can be a sign of overwatering or underwatering. Check the soil moisture and adjust your watering schedule accordingly. If the leaves are yellowing due to overwatering, repot the plant in fresh, well-draining soil.

### Brown Tips
Brown tips on the leaves can be a sign of dry air or low humidity. Increase the humidity around the plant by using a humidifier or placing it on a tray filled with water and pebbles.

### Spider Mites
Spider mites can infest Selaginella, causing yellowing leaves and fine webbing. Treat the plant with insecticidal soap or neem oil, and increase the humidity to prevent further infestations.

### Root Rot
Root rot can occur if the soil is waterlogged or the plant is overwatered. Repot the plant in fresh, well-draining soil, and adjust your watering schedule to prevent further problems.

### Slow Growth
Slow growth can be a sign of inadequate light or nutrients. Increase the light intensity or fertilize the plant with a balanced fertilizer to promote healthy growth.

## Propagation
To propagate Selaginella, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, making sure it has at least two nodes.
2. Remove lower leaves from the stem, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Plant the stem section in a well-draining potting mix, making sure the node is buried in the soil.
4. Water the soil gently but thoroughly, and provide high humidity and warm temperatures.
5. Roots should develop within 2-3 weeks, and the new plant can be transplanted into a larger pot.
