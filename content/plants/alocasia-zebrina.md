## Quick Care Summary
To care for your Alocasia zebrina, provide it with bright, indirect light, and water it weekly during the summer months and every 10-14 days during the winter. Maintain a consistent temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and keep the humidity level at a medium range. With its easy care requirements and non-toxic properties, the Alocasia zebrina is an excellent choice for beginners and experienced plant enthusiasts alike.

## Light Requirements
The Alocasia zebrina prefers bright, indirect light, but can tolerate partial shade. East- or west-facing windows are ideal, as they provide the right amount of light without exposing the plant to direct sunlight. A distance of 2-3 feet from the window is recommended to prevent scorching. If you're unable to provide natural light, you can supplement with grow lights, aiming for a light intensity of 200-400 foot-candles.

## Watering
To water your Alocasia zebrina, follow these steps:
1. Check the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water.
2. Water the plant thoroughly, making sure the pot drains well to prevent waterlogging.
3. Watering frequency will depend on the season: water every 7-10 days during the summer months and every 10-14 days during the winter months.
4. Check for signs of over-watering, such as yellowing leaves or soft, mushy stems.
5. Use room-temperature water, as cold water can shock the roots.

## Soil and Potting
A well-draining potting mix is essential for the Alocasia zebrina. A recipe of 2 parts peat, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark provides the right balance of moisture retention and drainage. Choose a pot that is slightly larger than the plant's root system, as this will help prevent waterlogging. Repotting is necessary every 2-3 years, as the plant outgrows its container. Signs that repotting is needed include the plant becoming top-heavy or the soil becoming depleted of nutrients.

## Fertilizing
Feed your Alocasia zebrina with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer during the growing season (spring-fall). Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to prevent burning the roots. A 20-20-20 NPK ratio is suitable for this plant. Fertilize every 2-3 weeks during the growing season, and skip fertilizing during the winter months when the plant is dormant.

## Humidity and Temperature
The Alocasia zebrina prefers a medium humidity level, ranging from 40-60%. To achieve this, you can:
1. Place the plant on a pebble tray filled with water, as the evaporating water will increase the surrounding humidity.
2. Group plants together, as they will create a microclimate with higher humidity levels.
3. Use a humidifier, especially in dry environments.
Maintain a consistent temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C), avoiding temperature fluctuations.

## Common Problems
### Yellowing Leaves
Symptom: Yellowing leaves can be a sign of over-watering or under-watering.
Cause: Over-watering can cause root rot, while under-watering can cause the leaves to dry out.
Fix: Adjust your watering schedule to ensure the soil is consistently moist but not waterlogged.

### Brown Edges
Symptom: Brown edges on the leaves can be a sign of dry air or over-fertilization.
Cause: Dry air can cause the leaves to become brittle and develop brown edges, while over-fertilization can burn the roots and cause the leaves to become discolored.
Fix: Increase the humidity level around the plant, and reduce fertilization to prevent burning the roots.

### Droopy Stems
Symptom: Droopy stems can be a sign of under-watering or root bound.
Cause: Under-watering can cause the stems to become weak and droopy, while root bound can cause the plant to become pot-bound and unable to absorb water and nutrients.
Fix: Adjust your watering schedule to ensure the soil is consistently moist, and consider repotting the plant into a larger container.

### Pests
Symptom: Pests such as spider mites, mealybugs, or scale can infest the plant.
Cause: Pests can be introduced to the plant through contaminated soil or infested plants.
Fix: Isolate the plant, and treat it with insecticidal soap or neem oil to eliminate the pests.

### Root Rot
Symptom: Root rot can cause the plant to become soft and mushy, with a foul odor.
Cause: Over-watering can cause root rot, as the excess water can cause the roots to become waterlogged and decay.
Fix: Stop watering the plant, and repot it into fresh, well-draining soil. Remove any affected roots to prevent the spread of the disease.

## Propagation
To propagate your Alocasia zebrina, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section with at least two nodes, as this will increase the chances of successful rooting.
2. Remove lower leaves, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Place the cut end in a glass of water or a propagation tray filled with water, making sure the node is submerged.
4. Keep the water level topped up, and change the water every 2-3 weeks to prevent bacterial growth.
5. Roots should develop within 2-3 weeks, at which point you can transfer the plant to a pot filled with well-draining soil.
The best time to propagate is during the spring or summer months, when the plant is actively growing.
