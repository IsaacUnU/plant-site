## Quick Care Summary

To care for your Schefflera actinophylla, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, about 3-4 feet away from an east- or west-facing window. Water your Schefflera actinophylla every 7-10 days during the summer and every 14 days during the winter, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and maintain a humidity level of 50-70%. With proper care, this plant is relatively easy to maintain, making it a great choice for those new to plant parenthood.

## Overview

The Schefflera actinophylla, native to Australia and New Guinea, is a tropical plant that has become a popular choice for indoor spaces due to its attractive, umbrella-like leaves and low-maintenance requirements. In its natural habitat, the Schefflera actinophylla grows as a tree, reaching heights of up to 40 feet, but when cultivated indoors, it typically remains much smaller, around 6-8 feet tall. The plant's ability to thrive in a variety of lighting conditions, from bright indirect light to partial shade, makes it an excellent choice for rooms with limited natural light.

The Schefflera actinophylla has also been recognized for its air-purifying properties, with a 1989 NASA Clean Air Study finding it effective at reducing formaldehyde, benzene, and trichloroethylene in enclosed spaces. This, combined with its humidity-boosting capabilities, makes it an excellent choice for those looking to create a healthier and more comfortable indoor environment.

## Light: The Importance of Bright, Indirect Light

The Schefflera actinophylla prefers bright, indirect light, but it can tolerate partial shade. The ideal light intensity for this plant is between 200-400 foot-candles. Placing the plant 3-4 feet away from an east- or west-facing window is ideal, as it will receive the right amount of indirect light. However, it's essential to avoid direct sunlight, as it can cause the leaves to become scorched or bleached.

In the winter months, when the sun is lower in the sky, it's best to move the plant closer to the window to ensure it receives enough light. Conversely, in the summer months, it's best to move it further away from the window to avoid too much direct sunlight.

## Watering: How to Water Your Schefflera actinophylla Without Drowning It

Watering your Schefflera actinophylla requires attention to detail to avoid overwatering. The best way to determine if the plant needs water is to stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water the plant thoroughly, making sure the pot drains well to prevent waterlogged soil.

The ideal watering frequency for the Schefflera actinophylla is every 7-10 days during the summer and every 14 days during the winter. However, this can vary depending on the humidity and temperature of the room.

## Soil & Repotting: The Importance of Well-Draining Soil

The Schefflera actinophylla prefers well-draining soil that is rich in organic matter. A mix of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark is ideal. This mix will provide the right amount of drainage and aeration for the roots.

Repotting the Schefflera actinophylla is necessary every 2-3 years, as the plant will outgrow its pot. The best time to repot is in the spring, when the plant is actively growing. Choose a pot that is only slightly larger than the previous one, as the plant prefers to be slightly root-bound.

## Fertilizing

Fertilizing your Schefflera actinophylla is essential to promote healthy growth. Use a balanced fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 10-10-10, and dilute it to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. Fertilize the plant monthly during the spring and summer months, when it is actively growing.

## Humidity and Temperature

The Schefflera actinophylla prefers a humidity level of 50-70% and a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). To achieve the right humidity level, you can place the plant on a pebble tray filled with water or use a humidifier. Avoid placing the plant near heating vents or cold windows, as this can cause temperature fluctuations that can be detrimental to the plant.

## Toxicity & Safety: The Importance of Keeping the Plant Out of Reach

The Schefflera actinophylla is toxic to cats and dogs if ingested, according to the ASPCA. If you suspect your pet has ingested any part of the plant, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435 immediately. Keep the plant out of reach of pets and children to avoid accidental ingestion.

### Yellowing Leaves

If the leaves of your Schefflera actinophylla are turning yellow, it's likely due to overwatering. Stop watering the plant for 2 weeks and allow the soil to dry out slightly. Check the soil moisture regularly to avoid overwatering in the future.

### Droopy Leaves

If the leaves of your Schefflera actinophylla are droopy, it's likely due to underwatering. Water the plant thoroughly, making sure the pot drains well to prevent waterlogged soil. Water the plant regularly, but avoid overwatering.

### Brown Tips

If the tips of the leaves of your Schefflera actinophylla are turning brown, it's likely due to too much direct sunlight. Move the plant to a spot with bright, indirect light, and use a sheer curtain to filter direct sunlight.

### Pests

If you notice any pests on your Schefflera actinophylla, treat the plant with insecticidal soap. Inspect the plant regularly for signs of pests, and take action immediately if you notice any.

### Root Rot

If you notice any signs of root rot, such as soft, mushy stems or a foul odor, repot the plant in well-draining soil. Avoid overwatering and ensure good drainage to prevent root rot in the future.

4. Water the plant thoroughly, and keep the soil moist but not waterlogged.

6. Roots should develop within 1-2 months, and the new plant can be transplanted into a larger pot.
