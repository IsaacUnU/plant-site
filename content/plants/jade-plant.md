## Quick Care Summary
The Jade Plant, scientifically known as Crassula ovata, thrives in bright indirect light with a minimum of 200-400 foot-candles. Water every 7-10 days by checking the soil depth or pot weight, ensuring the plant is not overwatered. Maintain humidity levels between 50-70% and temperatures within a range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). Fertilize with balanced 10-10-10 every month during spring and summer, reducing to once per season in fall and winter.

## Where This Plant Comes From
The Jade Plant, Crassula ovata, originates from South Africa. It is a succulent that has adapted over millennia to survive in arid conditions with minimal water and nutrients. Its thick, fleshy leaves store water and nutrients for long periods, making it well-suited to hot, dry climates. The plant's small, rounded leaves are a deep green color, often with a hint of red or purple on the undersides when grown in bright light.

## Light: What Works and What Doesn't
The Jade Plant prefers bright indirect light but can tolerate low to direct sunlight. Place it 3-5 feet from an east or south window for optimal growth, as these windows receive 200-400 foot-candles of light. If growing in a north-facing room, supplement with grow lights set to provide 180-250 foot-candles consistently.

| Factor | Requirement | Pro Tip |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Light | 200–400 foot-candles | Position away from direct sun, especially during summer months |

## Watering Without Overwatering
Overwatering is the most common mistake with Jade Plants. Check the soil depth by inserting a finger up to the first knuckle; if it's dry, water thoroughly until excess drains out. Alternatively, use the pot weight method: if the pot feels light, it needs watering.

### Step-by-Step Watering Plan
1. **Check Soil Depth**: Insert your finger 2 inches into the soil.
2. **Pot Weight Method**: If the pot is lighter than usual, water thoroughly.
3. **Watering Frequency**: Water every 7-10 days in spring and summer; reduce to once a month in fall and winter.

### Signs of Trouble
- **Wilting Leaves**: Overwatered or underwatered; check soil moisture.
- **Brown Tips**: Not enough humidity or direct sun exposure.
- **Soft, Mushy Stem Base**: Likely root rot from overwatering.

## The Right Soil Mix
A well-draining mix is crucial for Jade Plants. Use a combination of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark. This ratio ensures good drainage while providing necessary nutrients.

| Factor | Requirement | Pro Tip |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Soil | 60% potting mix + 30% perlite + 10% orchid bark | Use clay or ceramic pots with drainage holes |

## Fertilizing Schedule
Fertilize monthly during spring and summer using a balanced 10-10-10 formula. Dilute the fertilizer to half strength to prevent nutrient burn.

| Factor | Requirement | Pro Tip |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Fertilizer | Balanced 10-10-10, monthly spring–summer | Use half-strength solution |

## Humidity and Temperature
Maintain humidity levels between 50-70% using a pebble tray or humidifier. Keep temperatures within the range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). Avoid placing your Jade Plant near heating vents, cold windows, or drafty areas.

| Factor | Requirement | Pro Tip |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Humidity | 50–70% | Use a pebble tray or humidifier |

## Common Problems and Fixes
### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
- **Symptom**: Stem base feels soft or mushy.
- **Cause**: Overwatering leading to root rot.
- **Quick Fix**: Repot in fresh, well-draining soil; remove damaged roots if any.
- **Prevention**: Water only when the top inch of soil is dry.

### Wilting Leaves
- **Symptom**: Leaves look droopy or wilting.
- **Cause**: Underwatering or overexposure to direct sun.
- **Quick Fix**: Water thoroughly and move plant away from direct sunlight.
- **Prevention**: Check soil moisture regularly; avoid harsh sunlight exposure.

| Symptom | Most Likely Cause | Quick Fix | Prevention |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|------------|
| Soft, mushy stem base | Overwatering leading to root rot | Repot in fresh, well-draining soil; remove damaged roots if any. | Water only when the top inch of soil is dry. |
| Wilting leaves | Underwatering or overexposure to direct sun | Water thoroughly and move plant away from direct sunlight. | Check soil moisture regularly; avoid harsh sunlight exposure. |

## How to Propagate
Propagating Jade Plants is easy with stem cuttings. Take 3-4 inch segments, allow them to callous for a day, then place in well-draining soil. Keep the soil slightly moist and within a temperature range of 65-70°F (18-21°C) until rooted.

### Steps:
1. **Take Cuttings**: Use clean, sharp scissors to cut 3-4 inch segments.
2. **Allow Callousing**: Place cuttings on a paper towel for one day to dry out the wounds.
3. **Plant in Soil**: Insert cuttings into well-draining soil mix; keep slightly moist.
4. **Provide Light and Warmth**: Keep in bright, indirect light with temperatures between 65-70°F (18-21°C).
5. **Rooting Timeframe**: Expect roots to form within 3-4 weeks.

## Toxicity and Pet Safety
The Jade Plant is non-toxic to cats and dogs according to the ASPCA. However, ingestion can cause mild digestive issues such as vomiting or diarrhea. Place plants out of reach of pets to avoid accidental consumption.

| Factor | Requirement | Pro Tip |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Toxicity | Non-toxic to cats/dogs | Place out of reach of pets |

## Buying Guide
When purchasing a Jade Plant, look for healthy green leaves without any discoloration or browning. Roots should be firm and white, not black or mushy. Avoid plants with yellowing or drooping leaves as they may indicate overwatering.

### What to Look For:
- **Healthy Green Leaves**: No signs of discoloration.
- **Strong Roots**: Firm and white roots are a sign of good health.
- **No Yellow/Drooping Leaves**: Indicate potential overwatering issues.

### Red Flags:
- **Leaves with Brown Edges**: Often due to too much direct sun or underwatering.
- **Soft, Mushy Stem Base**: Signs of root rot from overwatering.
- **Yellowing Leaves**: May indicate poor drainage or soil mix issues.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: Can Jade Plants grow in low light conditions?**
A: While they prefer bright indirect light with 200-400 foot-candles, Jade Plants can tolerate lower light. However, growth will be slower and leaves may become lighter green.

**Q: How often should I fertilize my Jade Plant?**
A: Fertilize monthly during spring and summer using a balanced 10-10-10 formula. Reduce to once per season in fall and winter to avoid nutrient burn.

**Q: Why are the edges of my leaves turning brown?**
A: Brown leaf edges can be due to overexposure to direct sunlight or underwatering. Move your plant away from direct sun and ensure it is watered regularly but not overly so.
