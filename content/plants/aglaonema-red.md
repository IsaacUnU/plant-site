## Quick Care Summary

To care for your Aglaonema red, provide it with bright, indirect light, and water it once a week during the spring and summer months, reducing frequency to every 10-14 days in the fall and winter. Maintain a consistent temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and keep the humidity level at a moderate 40-60%. This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it perfect for beginners. With proper care, your Aglaonema red will thrive and become a beautiful addition to your indoor space.

## Light Requirements

The Aglaonema red prefers bright, indirect light, but can tolerate low light conditions. East- or west-facing windows are ideal, as they provide gentle, indirect light that won't scorch the leaves. Avoid placing the plant in direct sunlight, as this can cause the leaves to become scorched or discolored. If you're unsure about the lighting conditions in your home, you can use a foot-candle meter to measure the light intensity. The Aglaonema red prefers light levels between 200-400 foot-candles.

## Watering

To water your Aglaonema red, start by checking the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water the plant thoroughly, making sure the pot drains well to prevent waterlogged soil. During the spring and summer months, water the plant once a week, reducing frequency to every 10-14 days in the fall and winter. Avoid getting water on the leaves or crown of the plant, as this can cause rot and other problems.

## Soil and Potting

The Aglaonema red prefers a well-draining potting mix that contains a combination of peat, perlite, and orchid bark. A good mix might be 2 parts peat, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark. Avoid using regular potting soil, as it can be too dense and may cause waterlogging. When it comes to pot type, choose a pot that is slightly larger than the plant's root system, and has good drainage holes to prevent water from accumulating in the soil. Repot the plant every 2-3 years, or when the plant has outgrown its container.

## Fertilizing

To fertilize your Aglaonema red, use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 20-20-20. Apply the fertilizer once a month during the spring and summer months, reducing frequency to every 2-3 months in the fall and winter. You can also use a fertilizer that is specifically formulated for houseplants, as these products are often designed to provide the necessary nutrients for optimal growth and health.

## Humidity and Temperature

The Aglaonema red prefers a moderate humidity level, between 40-60%. To achieve this, you can use a humidifier or group the plant with other plants to create a microclimate. You can also place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles, as the evaporation of the water will increase the humidity around the plant. The ideal temperature range for the Aglaonema red is between 65-80°F (18-27°C), making it a great choice for indoor spaces.

## Common Problems

### Over-Watering
The symptom of over-watering is yellowing or droopy leaves, and the cause is too much water in the soil. To fix this, reduce the frequency of watering and make sure the pot drains well.

### Under-Watering
The symptom of under-watering is crispy or brown leaves, and the cause is not enough water in the soil. To fix this, increase the frequency of watering and make sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

### Pests
The symptom of pests is the presence of small, moving dots on the leaves or stems, and the cause is infestation by spider mites, mealybugs, or other pests. To fix this, use insecticidal soap or neem oil to treat the plant, and isolate it from other plants to prevent the infestation from spreading.

### Leaf Drop
The symptom of leaf drop is the sudden loss of leaves, and the cause is often a change in temperature, humidity, or light. To fix this, adjust the plant's environment to provide more stable conditions, and prune the plant to encourage new growth.

### Root Rot
The symptom of root rot is soft, mushy roots, and the cause is often over-watering or poor drainage. To fix this, reduce the frequency of watering and make sure the pot drains well, and consider repotting the plant in fresh, well-draining soil.

### Nutrient Deficiency
The symptom of nutrient deficiency is yellowing or discolored leaves, and the cause is a lack of essential nutrients in the soil. To fix this, fertilize the plant regularly, and consider using a fertilizer that is specifically formulated for houseplants.

## Propagation

To propagate your Aglaonema red, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, making sure it has at least two nodes.
2. Remove the lower leaves from the stem section, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Place the stem section in a glass of water or a propagation tray filled with water, and make sure the node is submerged.
4. Place the glass or tray in a bright, indirect light location, and keep the water level topped up.
5. Roots should develop within 1-2 weeks, and the new plant can be transferred to a pot filled with well-draining soil.
6. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged, and provide the new plant with bright, indirect light.
