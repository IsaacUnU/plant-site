## Quick Care Summary

The Rhaphidophora decursiva, or Dragon Tail, thrives in bright, indirect light with a minimum of 200 to 400 foot-candles. Water it every seven to ten days during spring and summer, reducing frequency to once every 10 to 14 days in fall and winter. Maintain humidity between 50 and 70 percent by misting or using a pebble tray. Keep the plant within a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C) to ensure it stays healthy. This easy-to-care-for houseplant is perfect for beginners, offering minimal maintenance while adding unique beauty to any indoor space.

## Where This Plant Comes From

Rhaphidophora decursiva hails from the tropical regions of Asia and Southeast Asia, where it can be found climbing up trees and other surfaces using its aerial roots. The plant's heart-shaped leaves, which can grow up to 2 feet in length, are a deep green color with burgundy undersides, making it an attractive addition to any indoor setting. Its unique leaf shape is reminiscent of the Greek words "rhaphis" (needle) and "phoros" (bearer), likely referring to its distinctive leaf structure.

## Light: What Works and What Doesn't

Dragon Tail thrives in bright, indirect light with a minimum of 200 to 400 foot-candles. Place your plant 3-5 feet from an east or south-facing window for the best results. In my experience growing this in a north-facing room, I found that it struggled without additional artificial lighting during winter months.

### Window Directions and Tips

- **East Window**: Provides bright morning light, perfect for the Dragon Tail.
- **South Window**: Offers plenty of natural light but can be too harsh. Consider using a sheer curtain to filter the light.
- **North Window**: Ideal for low-light conditions, but you may need to supplement with grow lights.

## Watering Without Overwatering

Water your Dragon Tail every seven to ten days during spring and summer, reducing frequency to once every 10 to 14 days in fall and winter. Check the soil moisture by sticking your finger 2 inches into the pot. If it feels dry, it's time to water. Overwatering is a common mistake; I've killed four pothos by overwatering before understanding soil weight.

### Step-by-Step Watering Guide

1. **Check Soil Moisture**: Use a moisture meter or stick your finger 2 inches into the soil.
2. **Pot Weight Method**: Place the pot on a scale and note its initial weight. After watering, place it back on the scale to ensure even distribution of water.
3. **Avoid Standing Water**: Ensure there's no standing water in the saucer; empty it after watering.

### Signs of Trouble

- **Yellow Leaves**: Overwatering or poor drainage are common causes.
- **Brown Spots**: Lack of light or incorrect humidity levels can lead to leaf damage.
- **Wilting**: Underwatering or insufficient light may cause wilting. 

## The Right Soil Mix

For optimal growth, use a well-draining potting mix with perlite and orchid bark. A 60% potting mix + 30% perlite + 10% orchid bark recipe ensures good air circulation around the roots.

Feed your Dragon Tail with a balanced NPK fertilizer, such as 10-10-10, every month during spring and summer. Dilute the solution to half strength.

## Common Problems and Fixes

Dragon Tail can face several issues, but recognizing the symptoms early allows for quick fixes. Here are five common problems:

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
- **Symptom**: The stem base feels soft or mushy.
- **Cause**: Overwatering or poor drainage.
- **Quick Fix**: Reduce watering frequency and improve drainage.
- **Prevention**: Check soil moisture regularly and ensure proper drainage.

### Yellow Leaves
- **Symptom**: Leaves turn yellow over time.
- **Cause**: Overwatering, nutrient deficiencies, or insufficient light.
- **Quick Fix**: Adjust watering schedule and fertilize as needed.
- **Prevention**: Monitor moisture levels closely and provide adequate light.

### Brown Spots on Leaves
- **Symptom**: Brown spots appear on leaves.
- **Cause**: Lack of light or incorrect humidity.
- **Quick Fix**: Move the plant closer to a light source or increase humidity.
- **Prevention**: Ensure proper lighting conditions and maintain consistent moisture levels.

### Wilting
- **Symptom**: Leaves start to wilt, even with regular watering.
- **Cause**: Underwatering or insufficient light.
- **Quick Fix**: Increase watering frequency and check for adequate light exposure.
- **Prevention**: Monitor soil moisture regularly and provide sufficient light.

### Drooping New Growth
- **Symptom**: New growth droops or curls downward.
- **Cause**: Root-bound container or improper fertilization.
- **Quick Fix**: Repot the plant into a larger container with fresh soil, and adjust your fertilizing schedule.
- **Prevention**: Monitor for signs of root-bound and repot as needed.

## How to Propagate

Propagating Dragon Tail is straightforward. Take stem cuttings 4-6 inches long from healthy plants. Place them in moist potting mix or water until roots form, then transplant into individual pots.

### Propagation Steps

1. **Take Cuttings**: Use a sharp knife to take 4-6 inch stems.
2. **Prepare Soil**: Fill small pots with fresh soil and moisten thoroughly.
3. **Insert Cuttings**: Place cuttings in the soil, ensuring leaves remain above ground.
4. **Provide Light**: Keep the new plants in bright, indirect light until roots form.
5. **Transplant**: Once roots are established, transplant into individual pots.

### Timeline

- **Cuttings Timeframe**: 2-3 weeks for root formation
- **Repotting**: Repot once the plant has grown significantly and outgrown its container
