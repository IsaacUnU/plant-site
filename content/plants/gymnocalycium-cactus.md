## Quick Care Summary
To care for your Gymnocalycium cactus, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, about 3 feet from an east or west window. Water it every 7-10 days during the summer and every 14 days during the winter, making sure the soil is dry to the touch between waterings. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and maintain a medium humidity level. This plant is easy to care for and can tolerate some neglect, making it perfect for busy people or those new to plant care.

## Light: The Importance of Bright, Indirect Light
The Gymnocalycium cactus requires bright, indirect light to photosynthesize and grow. The ideal light range for this cactus is between 200-400 foot-candles, which can be achieved by placing it near an east or west window. Direct sunlight can be too intense for the cactus, causing the skin to become scorched or discolored. To avoid this, use a sheer curtain to filter the sunlight and provide the cactus with the right amount of light.

## Watering: How to Water Your Gymnocalycium Cactus Without Drowning It
To water your Gymnocalycium cactus, stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water the cactus every 7-10 days during the summer and every 14 days during the winter. Make sure to use room-temperature water and avoid getting water on the cactus's body to prevent rot. The cactus is sensitive to overwatering, so it's better to err on the side of caution and underwater slightly.

## Soil & Repotting: The Importance of Well-Draining Soil
The Gymnocalycium cactus requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot and other problems. A good soil recipe for this cactus is a mix of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark. This mix provides good drainage and aeration, which is essential for the cactus's roots. Repot the cactus every 2-3 years to provide it with fresh soil and a larger pot if necessary. The best time to repot is during the spring when the cactus is actively growing.

## Fertilizing
Fertilize your Gymnocalycium cactus during the spring and summer months when it's actively growing. Use a balanced fertilizer with a ratio of 10-10-10, and dilute it to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. Apply the fertilizer once a month, and make sure to water the cactus thoroughly after fertilizing.

## Humidity and Temperature
The Gymnocalycium cactus prefers a medium humidity level, which can be achieved by placing the cactus on a tray filled with water and pebbles. The ideal temperature range for this cactus is between 65-80°F (18-27°C), which is typical for most indoor spaces. Avoid placing the cactus near heating vents or cold windows, as this can cause temperature fluctuations that can harm the cactus.

## Toxicity & Safety
The Gymnocalycium cactus is non-toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA. However, it's still important to keep the cactus out of reach of pets to avoid any potential harm. If your pet ingests any part of the cactus, it may experience gastrointestinal upset, including vomiting and diarrhea. If you suspect your pet has ingested the cactus, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435 for advice.

### Overwatering
Overwatering is one of the most common problems with the Gymnocalycium cactus. To fix this, stop watering the cactus immediately and repot it with fresh soil. Make sure to provide good drainage and aeration to prevent root rot.

### Underwatering
Underwatering can also be a problem, especially during the summer months when the cactus is actively growing. To fix this, adjust the watering schedule to provide more water, and make sure to provide a humid environment.

### Pests
Pests, such as mealybugs and spider mites, can infest the Gymnocalycium cactus. To fix this, treat the cactus with insecticidal soap and isolate it from other plants.

### Diseases
Fungal infections can also affect the Gymnocalycium cactus, especially in humid environments. To fix this, treat the cactus with fungicide and improve air circulation.

## Propagation
To propagate the Gymnocalycium cactus, follow these steps:
1. Choose a healthy offset from the mother plant.
2. Cut the offset from the mother plant using a sharp, sterile knife.
3. Allow the cut end to dry for a few days to form a callus.
4. Plant the offset in a small pot filled with well-draining soil.
5. Water the offset sparingly until it establishes itself, which can take several weeks.
6. Provide the offset with bright, indirect light and maintain a medium humidity level.
