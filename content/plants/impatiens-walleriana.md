## Quick Care Summary
To care for Impatiens walleriana, place it in a spot with indirect light, such as 2-3 feet from an east- or west-facing window, and maintain a temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C). Water your Impatiens walleriana every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. This plant is relatively easy to care for and can thrive in medium humidity, making it a great choice for beginners. With proper care, Impatiens walleriana can grow up to 12-18 inches tall and produce a profusion of colorful flowers.

## Light: The Window Direction That Determines Impatiens walleriana's Fate
Impatiens walleriana prefers bright, indirect light, but can tolerate low light conditions. The ideal foot-candle range for optimal growth is 200-400 foot-candles, which can be achieved by placing the plant 2-3 feet from an east- or west-facing window. In the Northern Hemisphere, a north-facing window is also a good option, as it provides indirect light without the intense heat of a south-facing window. During the winter months, when the days are shorter and the light is less intense, the plant may require more frequent watering to compensate for the reduced light.

## Watering: How to Water Impatiens walleriana Without Drowning It
To water Impatiens walleriana, use the following three-method soil moisture check:
1. Finger test: Stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle to check the moisture level.
2. Pot weight: Check the weight of the pot before and after watering to ensure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.
3. Moisture meter: Use a moisture meter to check the moisture level of the soil.

Water the plant every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. Use filtered or rainwater at room temperature to avoid shocking the roots. If the plant is overwatered, it can lead to root rot and other problems, so it's essential to monitor the soil moisture closely.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Soil Mix Makes Root Rot Rare
Impatiens walleriana prefers a well-draining potting mix that is rich in organic matter. A good soil recipe is 60% potting mix + 30% perlite + 10% orchid bark. This mix provides good drainage and aeration, while also retaining some moisture to prevent the soil from drying out too quickly.

Repot the plant every 1-2 years in the spring, using a pot that is only slightly larger than the previous one. This will help to prevent the soil from becoming too wet and will give the roots room to grow.

## Fertilizing
Impatiens walleriana benefits from regular fertilization, especially during the growing season. Use a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer, diluted to half the recommended strength, and apply it monthly during the spring and summer. This will provide the plant with the necessary nutrients to produce healthy growth and flowers.

## Humidity and Temperature
Impatiens walleriana prefers a medium humidity level, between 50-70%. To achieve this, you can use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles. This will help to maintain the right humidity level and prevent the plant from becoming too dry.

The ideal temperature range for Impatiens walleriana is 65-80°F (18-27°C). Keep the plant away from heating vents and cold windows to maintain a consistent temperature. Avoid placing the plant near air conditioning units or drafty windows, as this can cause the temperature to fluctuate and may damage the plant.

## Toxicity & Safety: Is Impatiens walleriana Safe for Pets?
According to the ASPCA, Impatiens walleriana is non-toxic to cats and dogs. However, it's still possible for pets to experience gastrointestinal upset if they ingest a large amount of the plant. If you suspect your pet has ingested Impatiens walleriana, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435 for advice.

To keep your pets safe, keep the plant out of reach and avoid placing it in areas where your pets tend to congregate. If you have pets that like to chew on plants, consider using a plant stand or hanging basket to keep the plant out of reach.

### Overwatering
If you notice that the stem base of your Impatiens walleriana is soft and mushy, it's likely that the plant is overwatered. To fix this, stop watering the plant and repot it in fresh, well-draining soil. Make sure to monitor the soil moisture closely and avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent further problems.

### Underwatering
If the leaves of your Impatiens walleriana are yellowing, it's likely that the plant is underwatered. To fix this, water the plant thoroughly and make sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. Check the soil moisture regularly and water as needed to prevent the plant from becoming too dry.

### Low Humidity
If the leaves of your Impatiens walleriana are droopy, it's likely that the plant is suffering from low humidity. To fix this, use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles to maintain a medium humidity level. Avoid placing the plant near heating vents or air conditioning units, as this can cause the humidity level to fluctuate and may damage the plant.

### Pests
If you notice pests on the leaves of your Impatiens walleriana, it's likely that the plant is infested. To fix this, use insecticidal soap or neem oil to treat the infestation. Inspect the plant regularly and isolate it if you notice any pests to prevent them from spreading to other plants.

### Slow Growth
If your Impatiens walleriana is not growing as quickly as you expect, it's likely that the plant is lacking nutrients. To fix this, fertilize the plant with a balanced fertilizer and make sure to provide it with the right amount of light and water. Fertilize the plant regularly during the growing season to promote healthy growth and flowering.

## Propagation
Impatiens walleriana can be propagated using stem cuttings. To propagate the plant, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem from the mother plant, just above a node.
2. Remove the lower leaves, leaving only the top two or three sets of leaves.
3. Dip the cut end of the stem in rooting hormone powder or liquid.
4. Plant the stem in a pot filled with a well-draining potting mix.
5. Water the plant thoroughly and keep the soil moist but not waterlogged.
6. Place the plant in a warm, bright location, but not in direct sunlight.
7. Roots should develop within 1-2 weeks, and the plant should be ready to transplant within 2-3 months.
