## Quick Care Summary

To care for your Pothos neon, place it in a spot with indirect light, water it once a week, and maintain a comfortable temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C). This plant is easy to care for, making it perfect for beginners. With its adaptability to different environments and low-maintenance needs, the Pothos neon is an excellent choice for those looking to bring some greenery into their space without too much fuss. It prefers medium humidity but can tolerate average indoor humidity levels, making it a versatile option for various settings.

## Overview

The Pothos neon, scientifically known as Epipremnum aureum, is a popular houseplant originating from the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands. It is known for its heart-shaped leaves with vibrant, neon-like variegation, which can range in color from yellow to white, adding a touch of elegance and uniqueness to any indoor space. The plant's ability to grow both in soil and as an epiphyte in its natural habitat makes it incredibly versatile. Its popularity as a houseplant stems from its ease of care, beautiful appearance, and air-purifying qualities, making it a favorite among plant enthusiasts and beginners alike.

Native to the tropical forests of the Solomon Islands and Vanuatu, the Pothos neon thrives in environments with high humidity and warm temperatures. However, it has adapted well to indoor conditions, making it a staple in many homes and offices. Its trailing stems can grow quite long, allowing it to cascade down from hanging baskets or climb up trellises, adding a decorative element to any room.

The Pothos neon's appeal also lies in its hardiness and ability to survive in less-than-ideal conditions, such as low light and infrequent watering, although it will thrive best with proper care. This resilience, combined with its attractive foliage, has made it a sought-after plant for those looking to bring a touch of nature indoors without requiring a lot of maintenance.

## Light Requirements

The Pothos neon prefers indirect light but can tolerate a range of lighting conditions, from low to bright indirect light. For optimal growth and to maintain its vibrant variegation, it should receive light that is filtered, such as through a sheer curtain. Direct sunlight can cause the leaves to become scorched, especially in warmer climates. A good rule of thumb is to place the plant near an east- or west-facing window, or about 5-10 feet away from a south-facing window to avoid direct sun. If the plant is receiving too much light, it may show signs such as yellowing leaves or brown spots, while too little light can cause the stems to become leggy and the variegation to fade.

## Watering

To water your Pothos neon, start by checking the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water the plant thoroughly until you see water coming out of the drainage holes, then allow the pot to drain completely to prevent waterlogged soil. The frequency of watering will depend on the season: water every 7-10 days during the spring and summer when the plant is actively growing, and reduce watering to every 4-6 weeks during the fall and winter when the plant is dormant. Signs of over-watering include yellowing leaves and a soft, mushy stem, while under-watering may cause the leaves to wilt and turn brown.

## Soil and Potting

The Pothos neon prefers a well-draining potting mix to prevent root rot. A recommended soil mix recipe is 2 parts peat moss, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark. This mix will provide the necessary nutrients and aeration for healthy root growth. For potting, choose a container that is only slightly larger than the plant's root ball to prevent the soil from becoming too wet. Repotting should be done every 1-2 years in the spring when the plant has outgrown its container. Signs that repotting is needed include the plant becoming top-heavy, roots growing out of the drainage holes, or the soil breaking down and no longer draining properly.

## Fertilizing

Feed your Pothos neon with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer during the growing season (spring and summer). A fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 20-20-20 is suitable. Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. Fertilize once a month, skipping the dormant season (fall and winter) when the plant requires fewer nutrients.

## Humidity and Temperature

The Pothos neon prefers a medium to high humidity environment, typically between 50-70%. However, it can adapt to average indoor humidity levels, which usually range from 40-50%. To maintain the ideal humidity, you can use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles, allowing the water to evaporate and increase the surrounding humidity. The plant thrives in temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C), which is typical for most indoor environments. Avoid placing the plant near heating or cooling vents, fireplaces, or drafty windows to maintain a stable temperature.

## Common Problems

### Over-Watering
Symptom: Yellowing leaves, soft and mushy stem
Cause: Too much water causing root rot
Fix: Reduce watering frequency, improve soil drainage, and consider repotting the plant in fresh, well-draining soil.

### Under-Watering
Symptom: Wilting leaves, dry and cracked soil
Cause: Insufficient water
Fix: Increase watering frequency, ensuring the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

### Loss of Variegation
Symptom: Leaves turning fully green
Cause: Insufficient light
Fix: Move the plant to a brighter location or supplement with grow lights.

### Pests
Symptom: Small, moving dots on the leaves or stems
Cause: Infestation by pests like mealybugs, spider mites, or scale
Fix: Isolate the plant, treat with insecticidal soap or neem oil, and improve air circulation.

### Root Bound
Symptom: Plant becoming top-heavy, roots growing out of the pot
Cause: The plant has outgrown its container
Fix: Repot the plant into a slightly larger container with fresh, well-draining soil.

### Leaf Drop
Symptom: Leaves falling off the plant
Cause: Sudden changes in temperature, humidity, or light
Fix: Provide a stable environment, and ensure the plant is receiving the right amount of water and nutrients.

## Propagation

1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, just above a node, using a clean, sharp knife or pruning tool.
2. Remove lower leaves from the cut section, leaving only 2-3 leaves at the top.
3. Place the cut end into a glass of water or a propagation tray filled with water, making sure the node is submerged.
4. Keep the water level topped up, changing the water every 2-3 days to prevent bacterial growth.
5. Roots should start to develop within 1-2 weeks. Once the roots are about an inch long, transfer the cutting to a pot filled with well-draining soil.
6. Water gently and provide indirect light. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged until the new plant is established.

The best time to propagate is during the spring and summer when the plant is actively growing. Expect the roots to develop within 1-3 weeks, and for the new plant to become established within 1-2 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I water my Pothos neon?**
Water your Pothos neon once a week during the spring and summer, reducing the frequency to every 4-6 weeks during the fall and winter.

**Is Pothos neon toxic to cats and dogs?**
No, the Pothos neon is non-toxic to cats and dogs, making it a safe choice for pet owners.

**Why are the leaves on my Pothos neon turning yellow?**
Yellow leaves can be a sign of over-watering, under-watering, or too much direct sunlight. Check your watering schedule, ensure the plant is receiving indirect light, and adjust your care routine accordingly.

**Can Pothos neon grow in low light?**
Yes, the Pothos neon can grow in low light conditions, although it may lose some of its variegation. For the best color, provide bright, indirect light.

**How big does Pothos neon get indoors?**
The Pothos neon can grow quite large indoors, with trailing stems reaching up to 8 feet long. Regular pruning can help maintain a desired size and encourage a fuller plant.
