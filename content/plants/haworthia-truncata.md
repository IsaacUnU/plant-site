## Quick Care Summary
To care for Haworthia truncata, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, about 3 feet from an east- or west-facing window. Water it every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure the soil is dry to the touch between waterings. This succulent prefers temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and can tolerate medium humidity levels. With its forgiving nature and low-maintenance requirements, Haworthia truncata is an excellent choice for beginners.

## Overview
Haworthia truncata, also known as Horse's Teeth, is a succulent species native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is known for its unique, truncated leaves that resemble horse's teeth. This plant has gained popularity as a houseplant due to its low-maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indoor conditions. Haworthia truncata is a small, slow-growing plant that can reach up to 6 inches in height and 8 inches in width. Its leaves are thick, fleshy, and have a bluish-green color with white or yellowish markings.

In its native habitat, Haworthia truncata grows in well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade. However, as a houseplant, it prefers bright, indirect light and consistent watering. This succulent is also known for its ability to purify the air, as shown in a 1989 NASA Clean Air Study, which found that Haworthia truncata is effective at reducing formaldehyde and other pollutants in enclosed spaces.

## Light: The Window Direction That Determines Haworthia truncata's Fate
Haworthia truncata prefers bright, indirect light, which can be achieved by placing it near an east- or west-facing window. The ideal foot-candle range for this plant is between 200-400 foot-candles. In the Northern Hemisphere, a south-facing window can also work, but it's essential to use a sheer curtain to filter the intense sunlight.

The distance from the window is also crucial, as Haworthia truncata can become scorched if it's too close to the glass. A distance of 3-4 feet from the window is recommended. Seasonal changes can also affect the plant's lighting needs, as the sun's intensity increases during the summer months. In the winter, the plant may require more light, which can be achieved by moving it closer to the window or using grow lights.

## Watering: How to Water Haworthia truncata Without Drowning It
Watering Haworthia truncata requires attention to detail, as overwatering can lead to root rot. The best way to check soil moisture is by sticking your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Another method is to check the pot's weight, as a dry pot will be significantly lighter than a waterlogged one.

The ideal watering frequency for Haworthia truncata is every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter. It's essential to use room-temperature water, as cold water can shock the roots. The plant can tolerate some fluoride in the water, but it's recommended to use filtered or rainwater to prevent any potential damage.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Mix for Haworthia truncata
Haworthia truncata prefers a well-draining soil mix that can prevent waterlogging. A mix of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark is recommended. This combination provides excellent drainage and aeration, which is essential for the plant's roots.

The best pot material for Haworthia truncata is terracotta, as it allows for better drainage and can help prevent waterlogging. The plant will need to be repotted every 2-3 years, as it outgrows its container. The signs that the plant needs repotting include roots growing out of the drainage holes or the soil becoming too compacted.

## Fertilizing
Haworthia truncata requires minimal fertilization, as it can thrive in poor soil conditions. However, a balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) can be applied during the growing season (spring-summer) to promote healthy growth. It's essential to dilute the fertilizer to half strength to prevent burning the roots.

## Humidity and Temperature
Haworthia truncata prefers a medium humidity level, which can be achieved by using a pebble tray or grouping plants. The ideal temperature range for this plant is between 65-80°F (18-27°C), which is typical for most indoor spaces. It's essential to keep the plant away from heating vents and cold windows to maintain a stable temperature.

## Toxicity & Safety: Is Haworthia truncata Toxic to Cats and Dogs?
According to the ASPCA, Haworthia truncata is non-toxic to cats and dogs. However, it's still possible for pets to experience gastrointestinal upset if they ingest a large amount of the plant. If you suspect your pet has ingested Haworthia truncata, it's essential to contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435.

To ensure the plant's safety, it's recommended to place it in a location where pets cannot access it. Haworthia truncata can be placed on a high shelf or in a hanging basket to keep it out of reach.

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
The most likely cause of a soft, mushy stem base is overwatering. To fix this issue, reduce the watering frequency and improve drainage by repotting the plant in a well-draining soil mix. It's also essential to avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent further damage.

### Yellowing Leaves
Yellowing leaves can be caused by underwatering. To fix this issue, increase the watering frequency, but make sure not to overwater. It's essential to check soil moisture regularly and adjust the watering schedule as needed.

### Brown, Crispy Leaves
Brown, crispy leaves can be caused by too much direct sunlight. To fix this issue, move the plant to a spot with bright, indirect light. Use a sheer curtain to filter intense sunlight and prevent further damage.

### Slow Growth
Slow growth can be caused by a lack of nutrients. To fix this issue, fertilize the plant with a balanced fertilizer during the growing season. Dilute the fertilizer to half strength to prevent burning the roots.

### Pests
Pests can infest Haworthia truncata, causing damage to the leaves and stems. To fix this issue, treat the plant with insecticidal soap or neem oil. Inspect the plant regularly and isolate it if necessary to prevent the infestation from spreading.

## Propagation
Propagating Haworthia truncata is relatively easy and can be done by leaf or offset division. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Choose a healthy leaf or offset with a well-developed root system.
2. Remove the leaf or offset from the mother plant, making sure to include a small amount of stem and roots.
3. Allow the cut end to dry for 1-2 days to form a callus.
4. Plant the leaf or offset in a well-draining soil mix, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.
5. Place the plant in a bright, indirect light spot and maintain a consistent temperature.
6. Water the plant sparingly, as it can be prone to rot if the soil is too moist.
