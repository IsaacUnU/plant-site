## Quick Care Summary
To care for Dudleya brittonii, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, ideally 200-400 foot-candles, and maintain a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). Water your Dudleya brittonii every 7-10 days during the summer and every 14 days during the winter, ensuring the soil is dry to the touch between waterings. This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a great option for those new to succulent care. With its low maintenance requirements and beautiful appearance, Dudleya brittonii can thrive in indoor conditions.

## Light: The Window Direction That Determines Dudleya brittonii's Fate
Dudleya brittonii prefers bright, indirect light, which is typically found near an east- or west-facing window. The ideal foot-candle range for optimal growth is 200-400 foot-candles. Placing the plant near a north-facing window may result in weak and spindly growth, while a south-facing window may cause the leaves to become scorched. The best window orientation in the Northern Hemisphere is east- or west-facing, as this provides the ideal amount of indirect light.

The distance from the window is also important, as placing the plant too close to the window can cause the leaves to become scorched. A distance of 3-5 feet (0.9-1.5 meters) from the window is recommended. The light needs of Dudleya brittonii change season-to-season, with more frequent watering required during the summer months when the plant is actively growing. During the winter months, the plant requires less water and can tolerate lower light conditions.

## Watering: How to Water Dudleya brittonii Without Drowning It
Watering Dudleya brittonii requires careful attention to the soil moisture levels. The best way to check the soil moisture is by sticking your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it is time to water. Another way to check the soil moisture is by lifting the pot and checking the weight. A dry pot will be much lighter than a wet one. A moisture meter can also be used to check the soil moisture levels.

The watering frequency of Dudleya brittonii varies depending on the season. During the summer months, the plant should be watered every 7-10 days, while during the winter months, it should be watered every 14 days. The water quality is also important, as Dudleya brittonii prefers room-temperature water that is free of fluoride. Tap water can be used, but it is recommended to use filtered or rainwater to prevent any potential damage to the plant.

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
A soft, mushy stem base is usually a sign of overwatering. To fix this problem, stop watering the plant and repot it in well-draining soil. Make sure to remove any dead or rotting roots and treat the plant with a fungicide to prevent further damage.

### Yellowing Leaves
Yellowing leaves are usually a sign of underwatering. To fix this problem, water the plant thoroughly and adjust the watering schedule. Make sure to check the soil moisture regularly to prevent underwatering in the future.

### Brown, Crispy Leaves
Brown, crispy leaves are usually a sign of too much direct sunlight. To fix this problem, move the plant to a spot with bright, indirect light. Use a sheer curtain to filter the sunlight and prevent further damage.

### White, Cottony Patches on the Leaves
White, cottony patches on the leaves are usually a sign of mealybugs. To fix this problem, treat the plant with insecticidal soap. Make sure to inspect the plant regularly for signs of pests and treat promptly to prevent further damage.

### Fine, White Webbing on the Leaves
Fine, white webbing on the leaves is usually a sign of spider mites. To fix this problem, treat the plant with insecticidal soap. Make sure to increase the humidity around the plant to prevent spider mites from thriving.

## Propagation
To propagate Dudleya brittonii, follow these steps:

1. Choose a healthy leaf from the mother plant and twist it gently to remove it from the stem.
2. Allow the leaf to dry for 1-2 days to form a callus over the wound.
3. Plant the leaf in well-draining soil and water sparingly.
4. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged, and provide bright, indirect light.
5. Roots and new leaves should develop within 1-2 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**How often should I water my Dudleya brittonii?**
Water your Dudleya brittonii every 7-10 days during the summer months and every 14 days during the winter months. Make sure to check the soil moisture levels regularly to prevent overwatering.

**Is Dudleya brittonii toxic to cats and dogs?**
According to the ASPCA, Dudleya brittonii is non-toxic to cats and dogs. However, it is still possible for pets to experience gastrointestinal upset if they ingest a large amount of the plant.

**Why are the leaves on my Dudleya brittonii turning yellow?**
Yellowing leaves on Dudleya brittonii are usually a sign of underwatering. Make sure to water the plant thoroughly and adjust the watering schedule to prevent further yellowing.

**Can Dudleya brittonii grow in low light?**
Dudleya brittonii prefers bright, indirect light, but it can tolerate low light conditions. However, the plant may become weak and spindly if it does not receive enough light.

**How big does Dudleya brittonii get indoors?**
Dudleya brittonii can grow up to 12 inches in height and 18 inches in width indoors, making it a great choice for small spaces. However, the plant can become larger if it is provided with optimal growing conditions and care.
