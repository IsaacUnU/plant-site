## Quick Care Summary
To care for your Crassula perforata, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, ideally 200-400 foot-candles, and maintain a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). Water your String of Buttons once a week during the summer and once every 10-14 days during the winter, making sure the soil is dry to the touch between waterings. Given its easy-going nature, this succulent is perfect for those new to plant care, requiring minimal maintenance and offering a beautiful display of trailing stems and unique, button-like foliage.

## Overview
The Crassula perforata, or String of Buttons, originates from South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. This succulent is part of the Crassulaceae family, known for its diverse range of species, many of which are popular houseplants due to their low-maintenance requirements and unique appearances. The String of Buttons gets its common name from its stems, which are adorned with small, round leaves that resemble buttons. It's a popular choice as a houseplant because it's easy to care for, can thrive in a variety of lighting conditions, and adds a decorative touch to any room with its trailing stems.

In its native habitat, Crassula perforata can be found growing in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it has adapted to survive with minimal water. This adaptation makes it an ideal plant for indoor spaces, where it can tolerate some neglect and still maintain its health and appearance. The scientific name, Crassula perforata, reflects its succulent nature, with "Crassula" referring to the thick, fleshy leaves and "perforata" describing the small, perforated appearance of the leaves.

The popularity of Crassula perforata as a houseplant can also be attributed to its small size and compact growth habit, making it perfect for shelves, hanging baskets, or small pots. It's a great choice for those looking to add some greenery to their space without committing to a large or high-maintenance plant. Additionally, its ability to purify the air and repel insects makes it a practical choice for improving indoor air quality and reducing the presence of unwanted pests.

## Light Requirements
Crassula perforata prefers bright, indirect light but can tolerate partial shade. For optimal growth, place it near an east- or west-facing window, where it can receive 200-400 foot-candles of light. Avoid direct sunlight, especially during the hottest part of the day, as it can cause the leaves to become scorched. Signs of too much light include leaves becoming discolored or developing brown spots, while too little light may result in stretched or leggy stems.

## Watering
To water your Crassula perforata, start by checking the soil moisture. Insert your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle; if the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water your plant once a week during the summer months when it's actively growing, and reduce this to once every 10-14 days during the winter when growth slows down. Use room-temperature water, and avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent rot. Signs of over-watering include soft, mushy stems and leaves, while under-watering may cause the leaves to shrivel and lose their plump appearance.

## Soil and Potting
For your Crassula perforata, a well-draining soil mix is essential to prevent waterlogged soil. A recommended mix is 2 parts peat, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark. This combination allows for good moisture retention while ensuring the roots don't sit in water. Choose a pot that is slightly larger than the plant's root system, as Crassula perforata prefers to be slightly root-bound. Repot your plant every 2-3 years in the spring when it becomes pot-bound, using a pot that is only one to two sizes larger than the previous one. Signs that repotting is needed include the plant drying out too quickly, visible roots on the surface, or the plant toppling over due to becoming too large for its container.

## Fertilizing
Feed your Crassula perforata during the growing season (spring and summer) with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer. Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to avoid burning the roots. A 20-20-20 NPK ratio is suitable, providing equal parts of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Fertilize your plant once a month, skipping the winter months when the plant is dormant.

## Humidity and Temperature
Maintain a medium humidity level around your Crassula perforata, ideally between 40-60%. To achieve this, you can use a humidifier, place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles, or group it with other plants to create a microclimate. The ideal temperature range is 65-80°F (18-27°C), with temperatures not dropping below 55°F (13°C) or exceeding 85°F (29°C). Avoid placing your plant near drafts or extreme temperature fluctuations.

## Common Problems
### Over-watering
Symptom: Soft, mushy stems and leaves.
Cause: Too much water, causing the roots to rot.
Fix: Stop watering immediately, and consider repotting the plant in fresh, well-draining soil. Remove any rotting parts to prevent the spread of disease.

### Under-watering
Symptom: Shrveled, dry leaves.
Cause: Insufficient water, causing the plant to dehydrate.
Fix: Increase the frequency of watering, ensuring the soil is moist but not waterlogged. Check the plant regularly to avoid over-correction.

### Pests
Symptom: Small, moving dots on the leaves or stems.
Cause: Infestation by pests such as mealybugs, spider mites, or scale.
Fix: Isolate the plant, and treat it with insecticidal soap or neem oil. Ensure good air circulation and maintain a clean environment to prevent re-infestation.

### Leaf Drop
Symptom: Leaves falling off the stems.
Cause: Could be due to over-watering, under-watering, or sudden changes in temperature or humidity.
Fix: Adjust the watering schedule, ensure the plant is in a stable environment, and consider pruning the plant to encourage new growth.

### Root Bound
Symptom: The plant is toppling over, or the soil is drying out too quickly.
Cause: The plant has outgrown its container and needs more room for its roots.
Fix: Repot the plant in a slightly larger container with fresh, well-draining soil. Prune the roots if necessary to fit the plant into its new pot.

## Propagation
1. Cut a stem from the mother plant, choosing one with at least two sets of leaves.
2. Remove the lower leaves, leaving only the top two sets of leaves.
3. Allow the cut end to dry for a day or two to form a callus, reducing the risk of rot.
4. Plant the cutting in a small pot filled with a well-draining soil mix, burying it up to the node where the leaves meet the stem.
5. Water sparingly until roots develop, which can take 1-3 weeks, depending on the conditions.
6. Once the new plant is established, treat it as you would a mature Crassula perforata, following the care guidelines outlined above.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**How often should I water my Crassula perforata?**
Water your Crassula perforata once a week during the summer and once every 10-14 days during the winter, ensuring the soil is dry to the touch between waterings.

**Is Crassula perforata toxic to cats and dogs?**
No, Crassula perforata is non-toxic to cats and dogs, making it a safe choice for pet owners.

**Why are the leaves on my Crassula perforata turning yellow?**
Yellowing leaves can be a sign of over-watering, under-watering, or too much direct sunlight. Adjust your care routine accordingly to prevent further leaf drop or discoloration.

**Can Crassula perforata grow in low light?**
While Crassula perforata can tolerate partial shade, it prefers bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Placing it in very low light conditions may lead to weak and leggy stems.

**How big does Crassula perforata get indoors?**
Crassula perforata can grow up to 12 inches tall and wide indoors, but it can be pruned to maintain a desired size and shape. Regular pruning also encourages the plant to produce more stems and leaves, enhancing its decorative appeal.
