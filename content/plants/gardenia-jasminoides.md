## Light: The Window Direction That Determines Gardenia jasminoides's Fate

Gardenia jasminoides prefers bright, indirect light, which can be provided by placing the plant 3-4 feet from an east- or west-facing window. The ideal foot-candle range for optimal growth is 200-400 foot-candles, while the minimum survival range is 100-200 foot-candles. In the Northern Hemisphere, a south-facing window can provide too much direct sunlight, which can cause the leaves to become scorched or bleached. In the winter months, when the sun is lower in the sky, a south-facing window can provide the necessary light for the plant.

## Watering: How to Water Gardenia jasminoides Without Drowning It

To check the soil moisture, use one of the following methods: (1) stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle, (2) lift the pot to check its weight, or (3) use a moisture meter. Water the plant every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. Use room-temperature water, and avoid getting water on the leaves or crown of the plant to prevent rot. If the plant is overwatered, stop watering for a few weeks and let the soil dry out slightly.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Soil Mix Makes Root Rot Rare

Use a well-draining potting mix that consists of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark. Avoid using regular potting soil, as it can retain too much water and cause root rot. Repot the plant every 2-3 years in the spring, using a pot that is only slightly larger than the previous one. Signs that the plant needs repotting include: (1) the soil is packed tightly around the roots, (2) the plant is top-heavy and prone to tipping over, or (3) the plant is showing signs of nutrient deficiency.

## Fertilizing

Fertilize the plant monthly during the spring and summer months with a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer. Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to prevent burning the plant's roots. Avoid fertilizing during the fall and winter months, as the plant is dormant and does not require as many nutrients.

## Humidity and Temperature

Maintain a medium humidity level of 50-70% around the plant, which can be achieved by using a humidifier or placing the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C), avoiding drafts and extreme temperature fluctuations. To achieve the right humidity, use one of the following methods: (1) place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles, (2) use a humidifier, or (3) group the plant with other plants to create a microclimate.

## Toxicity & Safety: Handling Gardenia jasminoides with Care

Gardenia jasminoides is mildly toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA. If ingested, the plant can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and lethargy in pets. If you suspect your pet has ingested Gardenia jasminoides, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435. ### Soft, Mushy Stem Base

If the stem base of your Gardenia jasminoides is soft and mushy, it is likely due to overwatering. To fix this, stop watering the plant for a few weeks and let the soil dry out slightly. Make sure to check the soil moisture regularly and avoid getting water on the leaves or crown of the plant.

### Yellowing Leaves

If the leaves of your Gardenia jasminoides are yellowing, it is likely due to underwatering. To fix this, water the plant thoroughly and make sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. Check the soil moisture regularly and adjust the watering schedule as needed.

### Droopy Leaves

If the leaves of your Gardenia jasminoides are droopy, it is likely due to low humidity. To fix this, use a humidifier or place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles. Maintain a medium humidity level of 50-70% around the plant.

### Pests on the Leaves

If you notice pests on the leaves of your Gardenia jasminoides, it is likely due to an infestation. To fix this, treat the plant with insecticidal soap or neem oil. Inspect the plant regularly for signs of pests and isolate the plant if necessary.

### Slow Growth

If your Gardenia jasminoides is not growing as quickly as you would like, it is likely due to a lack of nutrients. To fix this, fertilize the plant monthly during the spring and summer months. Use a balanced fertilizer and follow the instructions on the label.
