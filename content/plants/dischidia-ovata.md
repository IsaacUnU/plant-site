## Quick Care Summary
To care for Dischidia ovata, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, about 3 feet from an east or west window, and water it every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and maintain a medium humidity level of 50-60%. This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it perfect for beginners, and it's also non-toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA. ## Light: The Importance of Bright, Indirect Light
Dischidia ovata prefers bright, indirect light, which is typically provided by an east or west window. The ideal foot-candle range for this plant is 200-400 foot-candles, which is relatively low compared to other tropical plants. To provide the right amount of light, place the plant 3 feet from an east or west window, or use a sheer curtain to filter the light if it's too intense. Avoid placing the plant in direct sunlight, as this can cause the leaves to become scorched or bleached.

In the winter months, when the days are shorter and the light is less intense, you may need to adjust the plant's position to provide more light. You can also use grow lights to supplement the natural light, but be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions to avoid overlighting the plant.

## Watering: How to Water Dischidia ovata Without Drowning It
Dischidia ovata prefers to dry out slightly between waterings, which makes it relatively drought-tolerant. However, it's still important to water the plant regularly to prevent the soil from becoming too dry. The ideal watering frequency is every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter.

To check the soil moisture, stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. You can also use a moisture meter to check the soil moisture, but be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions.

When watering, use room-temperature water and avoid getting water on the plant's leaves or crown. Instead, water at the soil level, making sure to moisten the soil evenly. Avoid overwatering, as this can lead to root rot and other problems.

## Soil & Repotting: The Importance of Well-Draining Soil
Dischidia ovata prefers well-draining soil that's rich in organic matter. A good potting mix for this plant is 60% potting mix + 30% perlite + 10% orchid bark. This mix provides good drainage and aeration, which is essential for the plant's aerial roots.

When repotting, choose a pot that's only slightly larger than the previous one, as Dischidia ovata prefers to be slightly root-bound. Use a well-draining potting mix and gently remove the plant from its pot, taking care not to damage the aerial roots. Repot the plant in the spring or summer, when it's actively growing, and water thoroughly after repotting.

## Fertilizing
Dischidia ovata is a relatively low-feeding plant, but it will still benefit from regular fertilization. Use a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer, diluted to half the recommended strength, and apply it monthly during the spring and summer months. Avoid fertilizing during the winter months, when the plant is dormant.

## Humidity and Temperature
Dischidia ovata prefers a medium humidity level of 50-60%, which is relatively easy to maintain in most indoor spaces. To achieve the right humidity level, you can use a pebble tray or a humidifier. A pebble tray is a simple and effective way to increase the humidity around the plant, and it's also a great way to add some decorative stones or pebbles to the plant's pot.

In terms of temperature, Dischidia ovata prefers a range of 65-80°F (18-27°C), which is relatively moderate. Avoid placing the plant near heating vents or cold windows, as this can cause temperature fluctuations that may harm the plant.

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
If you notice that the stem base of your Dischidia ovata is soft and mushy, it's likely due to overwatering. To fix this, stop watering the plant immediately and allow the soil to dry out completely. You can also repot the plant in fresh, well-draining soil to prevent further root rot.

### Yellowing Leaves
If the leaves of your Dischidia ovata are turning yellow, it's likely due to underwatering. To fix this, water the plant thoroughly and adjust the watering schedule as needed. You can also check the soil moisture regularly to ensure that the plant is receiving the right amount of water.

### Droopy Leaves
If the leaves of your Dischidia ovata are droopy, it's likely due to low humidity. To fix this, use a humidifier or pebble tray to increase the humidity around the plant. You can also group the plant with other plants to create a microclimate with higher humidity.

### Pests on Leaves
If you notice pests on the leaves of your Dischidia ovata, it's likely due to an infestation. To fix this, treat the plant with insecticidal soap or neem oil, and inspect the plant regularly to prevent further infestations.

### Slow Growth
If your Dischidia ovata is growing slowly, it's likely due to a lack of light. To fix this, move the plant to a brighter location or use grow lights to supplement the natural light. You can also adjust the watering schedule and fertilization to promote healthy growth.

## Propagation
Dischidia ovata can be propagated using stem cuttings or leaf cuttings. To propagate using stem cuttings, follow these steps:

1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, making sure to include at least two nodes.
2. Remove the lower leaves from the cutting, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Dip the cut end of the stem in rooting hormone powder or liquid.
4. Plant the cutting in a pot filled with well-draining soil, and water thoroughly.
5. Place the pot in a warm, humid location, and keep the soil moist but not waterlogged.
6. Roots should develop within 1-2 months, and the new plant can be transplanted into a larger pot.
