## Quick Care Summary

Haworthia cooperi, or Window Haworthia, thrives in indirect light with 200-400 foot-candles, ideally positioned 3-5 feet from an east or south window. Water it every 7-10 days during summer and reduce to once every 10-14 days in winter. Maintain a temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) with medium humidity—aim for 50-70%. It's non-toxic, making it safe for pets; however, keep away from cats and dogs that might chew on it. Its growth rate is moderate, adding about a half-inch per year.

## Where This Plant Comes From

Haworthia cooperi originates from the Eastern Cape Province of South Africa. In its natural habitat, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade or full sun. The distinct white, translucent tips on its leaves are a result of exposure to direct sunlight, which helps prevent excessive evaporation in the arid environment.

## Light: What Works and What Doesn't

Window Haworthia loves bright, indirect light—specifically 200-400 foot-candles. Position your plant within 3-5 feet of an east or south window to mimic its natural environment. Too much direct sunlight can cause the leaves to burn, while insufficient light leads to pale and leggy growth.

### Tips for Lighting

1. **East Window:** Ideal from late morning until mid-afternoon.
2. **South Window:** Best in early morning and late afternoon; avoid peak sun hours.
3. **North Window:** If you have a north-facing room, supplement with grow lights set to 40-60 watts.

## Watering Without Overwatering

Water your Window Haworthia every 7-10 days during the summer months, reducing frequency in winter when it's less active. Here’s how to ensure proper watering:

### Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Check Soil Moisture:** Insert a finger into the soil up to the first knuckle; if dry, water.
2. **Water Thoroughly:** Pour water directly onto the soil until you see excess moisture drain from the bottom of the pot.
3. **Allow Drainage:** Ensure the saucer is empty after 15-20 minutes.

### Signs of Trouble
- **Wilting Leaves:** Often due to underwatering; check for dryness in the soil.
- **Soft, Mushy Stem Base:** Overwatering or poor drainage; adjust watering schedule and ensure proper drainage.
- **Brown Tips on Leaves:** Incorrect humidity levels; consider increasing humidity around your plant.

## The Right Soil Mix

A well-draining mix is key to keeping your Haworthia cooperi healthy. Use a 60% potting soil, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark blend:

### Exact Recipe
- **Potting Soil:** Standard potting mix.
- **Perlite:** Improves drainage by about 30%.
- **Orchid Bark:** Adds air pockets for root respiration.

## Fertilizing Schedule

Fertilize your Window Haworthia with a balanced 10-10-10 NPK fertilizer monthly during the growing season (spring and summer). Dilute to half strength before application. Signs of deficiency include stunted growth, yellowing leaves, and lacklustre appearance.

## Humidity and Temperature

Maintain humidity between 50-70%. Use a pebble tray filled with water or place your plant near a humidifier for optimal conditions. Avoid placing it too close to cold windows or heating vents, which can cause temperature fluctuations that stress the plant.

### Actionable Tips
1. **Pebble Tray:** Fill a saucer with pebbles and add enough water so only the bottom of the pot sits in the water.
2. **Misting:** Spritz the leaves once daily during dry spells.
3. **Humidifier:** A small humidifier can increase ambient humidity.

## Common Problems and Fixes

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
**Cause:** Overwatering or poor drainage.
**Quick Fix:** Reduce watering frequency; repot in a well-draining mix if necessary.
**Prevention:** Ensure your pot has proper drainage holes and use the 60/30/10 soil mix.

### Wilting Leaves
**Cause:** Underwatering or incorrect light conditions.
**Quick Fix:** Increase watering frequency but ensure good drainage; move to better-lit location.
**Prevention:** Monitor soil moisture regularly and adjust watering as needed.

## How to Propagate

Propagating Haworthia cooperi is straightforward—just follow these steps:

1. **Wait for Pups:** New plants (pups) will grow from the mother plant’s base.
2. **Separate Pups:** Gently remove pups with a clean, sharp knife or scissors.
3. **Plant Individually:** Place each pup in its own pot filled with the 60/30/10 soil mix.
4. **Water Slightly:** Water lightly to help establish roots.

### Timeline and Success Tips
- **Timeline:** Pups can be separated after 6 months of growth.
- **Success Tips:** Ensure pups have established roots before repotting; keep them in bright, indirect light.

## Toxicity and Pet Safety

Haworthia cooperi is non-toxic to cats and dogs according to the ASPCA. However, it’s best to keep it out of reach of pets that might chew on it due to its succulent leaves.

### Exact Toxicity Level
- **Toxic to Cats:** Non-toxic.
- **Toxic to Dogs:** Non-toxic.
- **ASPCA Reference:** "Haworthia cooperi is safe for household use and can be enjoyed by pet owners."

## Buying Guide

When purchasing a Window Haworthia, look for these signs of a healthy plant:

### What to Look For
1. **Healthy Green Color:** Avoid yellow or brown leaves.
2. **Sturdy Stem Base:** Ensure the base is firm and not soft.
3. **Pup Presence:** Healthy plants often have small pups growing from the mother plant.

### Red Flags
- **Wilting Leaves:** Indicates overwatering or underwatering.
- **Soft, Mushy Stem:** Overwatering; may need repotting.
- **Brown Tips on Leaves:** Incorrect humidity levels; consider your environment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Q: Can I use a different type of potting soil?**
   A: Stick to the recommended 60/30/10 mix for best results, but if you must change, ensure it is well-draining with good air circulation.
   
2. **Q: How do I know when my plant needs repotting?**
   A: Repot every two years or when the roots start to grow out of the drainage holes.

3. **Q: Can I move my Window Haworthia outside during summer?**
   A: Yes, but acclimate it slowly by gradually exposing it to outdoor conditions over a week or so.

4. **Q: How often should I mist my plant?**
   A: Misting once daily is sufficient for most indoor environments; more frequent in very dry climates.

5. **Q: My leaves are turning yellow—what should I do?**
   A: Check the soil moisture and drainage. Yellowing can be due to overwatering or nutrient deficiency. Adjust watering and fertilization accordingly.
