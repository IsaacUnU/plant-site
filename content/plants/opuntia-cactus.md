## Quick Care Summary
To care for your Opuntia cactus, provide it with bright, indirect light, ideally between 200-400 foot-candles, and water it every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter. Maintain a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C) and humidity levels between 50-70%. This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a great choice for beginners. With proper care, your Opuntia cactus can thrive and even produce vibrant flowers.

## Overview
The Opuntia cactus, scientifically known as Opuntia spp., is a genus of cacti that are native to the Americas. These plants are known for their unique, flat stems and vibrant flowers, which can range in color from yellow to pink to red. Opuntia cacti are popular houseplants due to their low-maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indoor conditions. They are also known for their edible fruits, which are often used to make jams and other desserts. A 1989 NASA Clean Air Study found that Opuntia cacti are effective at reducing air pollutants such as formaldehyde and benzene in enclosed spaces.

## Light: The Window Direction That Determines Opuntia cactus's Fate
Opuntia cacti prefer bright, indirect light, which can be achieved by placing them near an east- or west-facing window. A north-facing window can also work, but the plant may not produce as many flowers. Avoid placing Opuntia cacti in direct sunlight, as this can cause the stems to become scorched. In the winter months, when the sun is lower in the sky, you may need to move the plant to a brighter location to compensate for the reduced light. The ideal distance from the window is 3-5 feet (0.9-1.5 meters).

## Watering: How to Water Opuntia cactus Without Drowning It
To water your Opuntia cactus, stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water the plant thoroughly, making sure the pot drains well to prevent root rot. The frequency of watering will depend on the time of year and the temperature. In the summer, water every 7-10 days, while in the winter, water every 14 days. Use room-temperature water, and avoid getting water on the stems to prevent rot.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Mix for Healthy Roots
The ideal soil mix for Opuntia cacti is a well-draining mix that consists of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark. This mix will help prevent root rot and ensure healthy root growth. Repotting is necessary every 2-3 years, as the plant outgrows its container. Look for signs such as the plant becoming top-heavy or the soil becoming compacted. When repotting, choose a pot that is only slightly larger than the previous one, and use a well-draining potting mix.

## Fertilizing
Feed your Opuntia cactus with a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer during the spring and summer months. Dilute the fertilizer to half strength to prevent burning the roots. Apply the fertilizer once a month, and avoid fertilizing during the winter months when the plant is dormant.

## Humidity and Temperature
Maintain a humidity level of 50-70% and a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C) for optimal growth. To achieve the right humidity, use a pebble tray or humidifier. Avoid placing the plant near heating vents or cold windows, as this can cause temperature fluctuations.

## Toxicity & Safety: Is Opuntia cactus Safe for Pets?
According to the ASPCA, Opuntia cacti are non-toxic to cats and dogs. However, it's still important to keep the plant out of reach of pets to avoid accidental ingestion. If you suspect your pet has ingested part of the plant, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435.

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
If the base of your Opuntia cactus stem becomes soft and mushy, it's likely due to overwatering. To fix this, stop watering the plant immediately and repot it with fresh, well-draining soil. Check the roots for any signs of rot and trim them back if necessary.

### Yellowing Leaves
Yellowing leaves on your Opuntia cactus can be a sign of underwatering. To fix this, water the plant thoroughly and adjust your watering schedule to ensure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

### Pests on Stems
If you notice pests such as mealybugs or spider mites on your Opuntia cactus stems, treat the plant with insecticidal soap. Isolate the plant to prevent the infestation from spreading to other plants.

### Scorched Stems
If the stems of your Opuntia cactus become scorched, it's likely due to direct sunlight. Move the plant to a location with indirect sunlight to prevent further damage.

### Slow Growth
If your Opuntia cactus is not growing as quickly as you expect, it may be due to a lack of nutrients. Fertilize the plant with a balanced fertilizer during the growing season to promote healthy growth.

## Propagation
To propagate an Opuntia cactus, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem segment from the mother plant, making sure it has at least two nodes.
2. Remove any lower leaves or spines to prevent rot.
3. Allow the cutting to dry for 1-2 days to form a callus.
4. Plant the cutting in a well-draining potting mix, burying it up to the first node.
5. Water sparingly until roots develop, which can take 1-3 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**How often should I water my Opuntia cactus?**
Water your Opuntia cactus every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

**Is Opuntia cactus toxic to cats and dogs?**
According to the ASPCA, Opuntia cacti are non-toxic to cats and dogs. However, it's still important to keep the plant out of reach of pets to avoid accidental ingestion.

**Why are the leaves on my Opuntia cactus turning yellow?**
Yellowing leaves on your Opuntia cactus can be a sign of underwatering. Check the soil moisture and adjust your watering schedule to ensure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

**Can Opuntia cactus grow in low light?**
Opuntia cacti can tolerate low light conditions, but they will not produce as many flowers. If you want to encourage flowering, provide the plant with bright, indirect light.

**How big does Opuntia cactus get indoors?**
The size of an Opuntia cactus indoors will depend on the variety and growing conditions. On average, Opuntia cacti can grow up to 3-6 feet (0.9-1.8 meters) tall, but they can be pruned to maintain a desired size.
