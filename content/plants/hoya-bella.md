## Quick Care Summary
To care for your Hoya bella, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, such as 3 feet from an east-facing window, which receives 200-400 foot-candles of light. Water your Hoya bella every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure to check the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and maintain a medium humidity level of 50-70%. This plant is relatively easy to care for and can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a great choice for beginners.

## Light: The Key to Hoya bella's Success
The Hoya bella prefers bright, indirect light, but can tolerate low light conditions. For optimal growth, place your Hoya bella 3 feet from an east-facing window, which receives 200-400 foot-candles of light. In the winter months, when the days are shorter and the light is less intense, you may need to move your Hoya bella to a brighter location to keep it happy. Be careful not to place your Hoya bella in direct sunlight, as this can cause the leaves to become scorched and the plant to become stressed.

## Watering: The Delicate Balance
To water your Hoya bella, check the soil moisture by inserting your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water. Water your Hoya bella every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter. Make sure to use room-temperature water, as cold water can shock the roots and cause the plant to become stressed. Avoid getting water on the leaves or crown of the plant, as this can cause rot and kill the plant.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Mix
The Hoya bella prefers a well-draining potting mix that is rich in organic matter. A good mix would be 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark. This mix will provide good drainage and aeration, while also retaining enough moisture to keep the plant happy. Repot your Hoya bella every 2-3 years, using a pot that is only slightly larger than the previous one. This will give the roots enough room to grow, while also preventing the plant from becoming too large.

## Fertilizing: Feeding Your Hoya bella
To fertilize your Hoya bella, use a balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer during the spring and summer months. Dilute the fertilizer to half strength to prevent burning the roots. Feed your Hoya bella once a month, taking care not to overfertilize. This will provide the plant with the nutrients it needs to produce beautiful, fragrant flowers.

## Humidity and Temperature: The Perfect Environment
The Hoya bella prefers a medium humidity level of 50-70%. To achieve this, you can use a pebble tray or humidifier. Place the plant on a tray filled with water and pebbles, or use a humidifier to maintain the ideal humidity level. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C), avoiding extreme temperatures that can cause the plant to become stressed.

## Toxicity & Safety: Keeping Your Pet Safe
The Hoya bella is non-toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA. However, it's still important to keep the plant out of reach of your pets, as they may still try to ingest it. ### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
If you notice that the stem base of your Hoya bella is soft and mushy, it's likely due to overwatering. To fix this, stop watering the plant immediately and repot it in fresh, well-draining soil. Make sure to check the soil moisture regularly to avoid overwatering in the future.

### Yellowing Leaves
If the leaves of your Hoya bella are turning yellow, it's likely due to underwatering. To fix this, increase the watering frequency and make sure to check the soil moisture regularly. You can also try fertilizing the plant with a balanced fertilizer to provide it with the nutrients it needs.

### Pests on the Plant
If you notice pests on your Hoya bella, such as mealybugs or spider mites, it's likely due to an infestation. To fix this, treat the plant with insecticidal soap or neem oil, and make sure to isolate it from other plants to prevent the infestation from spreading.

### Slow Growth
If your Hoya bella is not growing as quickly as you expect, it's likely due to a lack of nutrients. To fix this, fertilize the plant with a balanced fertilizer during the growing season, and make sure to provide it with the right amount of light and water.

### Dropping Leaves
If the leaves of your Hoya bella are dropping, it's likely due to extreme temperatures. To fix this, move the plant to a more stable location, away from heating vents and cold windows. Make sure to keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) to prevent the plant from becoming stressed.

## Propagation
To propagate a Hoya bella, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, making sure to include at least two nodes.
2. Remove the lower leaves from the stem section, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Place the stem section in a pot filled with a well-draining potting mix, and water thoroughly.
4. Keep the soil moist and warm, and provide the plant with bright, indirect light.
5. Roots should develop within 1-2 months, and the new plant can be transplanted into a larger pot.
