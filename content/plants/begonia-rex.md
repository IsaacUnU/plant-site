## Quick Care Summary
To care for your Begonia rex, place it in a spot with bright, indirect light, such as 3-4 feet from an east- or west-facing window. Water your Begonia rex every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged. Keep the temperature between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and maintain a medium humidity level of 50-70%. With proper care, your Begonia rex will thrive and provide you with beautiful, unique leaves. As an added bonus, a 1989 NASA Clean Air Study found that Begonia rex is effective at reducing air pollutants like formaldehyde in enclosed spaces.

## Overview
The Begonia rex, or Painted Begonia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Begoniaceae. Native to the tropical regions of Asia, this plant has been cultivated for its stunning, unique leaves that showcase a variety of colors and patterns. The Begonia rex is a popular houseplant due to its moderate growth rate, beautiful foliage, and relatively low maintenance care requirements. In its natural habitat, the Begonia rex can be found growing on rocky outcrops and in shaded, humid areas. As a houseplant, it can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a great choice for plant enthusiasts of all levels.

The Begonia rex is a relatively small plant, typically growing to be around 12-18 inches tall and wide. Its leaves are the main attraction, with intricate patterns and colors that can range from deep greens to vibrant silvers and pinks. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring and summer months, but these are often overshadowed by the beauty of the leaves. With proper care, the Begonia rex can be a stunning addition to any room, providing a touch of tropical elegance and a unique conversation piece.

## Light: The Window Direction That Determines Begonia rex's Fate
The Begonia rex prefers bright, indirect light, with a foot-candle range of 200-400. In the Northern Hemisphere, a north- or east-facing window is ideal, as it provides the right amount of indirect light without the risk of direct sunlight. The plant should be placed at least 3-4 feet away from the window to prevent scorching or discoloration. In the winter months, the Begonia rex may require more light, as the days are shorter and the sun is lower in the sky. A south-facing window can be used during this time, but be sure to provide some shade to prevent scorching.

Exact visual symptoms of too much light include bleached or scorched leaves, while too little light can result in pale or etiolated growth. To prevent these issues, monitor your plant's response to light and adjust its position accordingly. If you notice the leaves becoming scorched or discolored, move the plant to a spot with more indirect light. If the leaves are becoming pale or etiolated, move the plant to a spot with more direct light.

## Watering: How to Water Begonia rex Without Drowning It
To water your Begonia rex, use the following three-method soil moisture check:
1. Finger test: Stick your finger into the soil up to the first knuckle. If the soil feels dry, it's time to water.
2. Pot weight: Lift the pot and compare its weight to when it was last watered. If the pot feels light, it's time to water.
3. Moisture meter: Use a moisture meter to check the soil moisture levels. If the meter reads dry or low, it's time to water.

Water your Begonia rex every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter. Use room temperature water, and avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent spotting or discoloration. If you notice the plant is becoming waterlogged or developing root rot, reduce the frequency of watering and improve drainage by adding more perlite or sand to the soil.

## Soil & Repotting: The Right Soil Mix Makes Root Rot Rare
The Begonia rex prefers a well-draining soil mix, with a ratio of 60% potting mix, 30% perlite, and 10% orchid bark. This mix provides the right amount of moisture and drainage for the plant's roots. Terracotta pots are ideal, as they allow for good drainage and can help prevent root rot.

Exact signs that the plant needs repotting include:
- The plant has outgrown its pot and needs more room to grow.
- The soil is breaking down and needs to be refreshed.
- The plant is showing signs of nutrient deficiency, such as yellowing leaves or stunted growth.

To repot your Begonia rex, follow these steps:
1. Choose a pot that is only slightly larger than the current one.
2. Prepare the new pot by adding a layer of fresh soil mix.
3. Gently remove the plant from its current pot and inspect the roots.
4. Trim any dead or damaged roots, and gently spread out the remaining roots.
5. Place the plant in the new pot and add fresh soil mix, making sure to leave enough space for watering.
6. Water the plant thoroughly and provide bright, indirect light.

## Fertilizing
Fertilize your Begonia rex with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer (10-10-10 NPK) during the growing season (spring and summer). Dilute the fertilizer to half the recommended strength to prevent burning the roots. Apply the fertilizer once a month, making sure to water the plant thoroughly before and after application.

## Humidity and Temperature
The Begonia rex prefers a medium humidity level of 50-70% and a temperature range of 65-80°F (18-27°C). To achieve the right humidity level, you can use the following methods:
- Place the plant on a pebble tray filled with water, which will increase the humidity around the plant as the water evaporates.
- Group plants together, which will create a microclimate with higher humidity.
- Use a humidifier, which will add moisture to the air and maintain a consistent humidity level.

## Toxicity & Safety: Safe Placement Recommendations
The Begonia rex is non-toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA. However, it's still important to keep the plant out of reach of pets and children to prevent accidental ingestion. If you suspect your pet has ingested any part of the plant, contact the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435 for advice.

### Soft, Mushy Stem Base
If you notice the stem base of your Begonia rex becoming soft and mushy, it's likely due to overwatering. To fix this issue, reduce the frequency of watering and improve drainage by adding more perlite or sand to the soil. You can also try repotting the plant in fresh soil to prevent the spread of root rot.

### Yellowing Leaves
Yellowing leaves can be a sign of underwatering, especially if the soil is dry to the touch. To fix this issue, increase the frequency of watering and check the soil moisture regularly. You can also try adjusting the watering schedule to ensure the plant is receiving enough moisture.

### Scorched or Discolored Leaves
If you notice the leaves of your Begonia rex becoming scorched or discolored, it's likely due to too much direct sunlight. To fix this issue, move the plant to a spot with more indirect light, such as a north- or east-facing window. You can also try using a sheer curtain to filter the sunlight and prevent scorching.

### Pale or Etiolated Growth
Pale or etiolated growth can be a sign of too little light, especially if the plant is not receiving enough direct sunlight. To fix this issue, move the plant to a spot with more direct light, such as a south-facing window. You can also try using grow lights to supplement the natural light and promote healthy growth.

### Root Rot
Root rot can be a serious issue for the Begonia rex, especially if the soil is waterlogged or poorly draining. To fix this issue, reduce the frequency of watering and improve drainage by adding more perlite or sand to the soil. You can also try repotting the plant in fresh soil to prevent the spread of root rot.

## Propagation: Step-by-Step Guide
To propagate your Begonia rex, follow these steps:
1. Cut a stem section from the mother plant, making sure to include at least two nodes.
2. Remove the lower leaves from the stem section, leaving only the top two or three leaves.
3. Dip the cut end of the stem section in rooting hormone powder or liquid.
4. Plant the stem section in a pot filled with a well-draining soil mix, making sure to bury the node (the joint where the leaf meets the stem).
5. Water the soil gently but thoroughly, and provide bright, indirect light.
6. Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged, and maintain a temperature of 65-80°F (18-27°C).
7. Roots should develop within 1-2 weeks, and new growth should appear within 2-3 weeks.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**How often should I water my Begonia rex?**
Water your Begonia rex every 7-10 days in the summer and every 14 days in the winter, making sure the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

**Is Begonia rex toxic to cats and dogs?**
No, the Begonia rex is non-toxic to cats and dogs, according to the ASPCA.

**Why are the leaves on my Begonia rex turning yellow?**
Yellowing leaves can be a sign of underwatering, especially if the soil is dry to the touch. Increase the frequency of watering and check the soil moisture regularly.

**Can Begonia rex grow in low light?**
While the Begonia rex can tolerate low light conditions, it will not thrive in very low light. It prefers bright, indirect light, with a foot-candle range of 200-400.

**How big does Begonia rex get indoors?**
The Begonia rex is a relatively small plant, typically growing to be around 12-18 inches tall and wide. It can be pruned to maintain a desired size and shape.
